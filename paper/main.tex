\begin{abstract}
Federated learning (FL) on O-RAN edge gNBs is a natural candidate for slice-level SLA-violation forecasting when raw per-user telemetry is undesirable or infeasible to pool centrally (FL itself does not provide formal privacy guarantees; see Section~\ref{sec:limits} L16). Yet the deployment design space --- sequence backbone, federation algorithm, client-heterogeneity partition, commodity edge hardware --- has been studied one axis at a time, leaving the joint behaviour under realistic O-RAN traffic characterised only anecdotally. This paper presents the first cross-architecture empirical FL benchmark on the public Colosseum/ColO-RAN testbed: 3 backbones (LSTM, Mamba, Spiking-SSM) $\times$ 5 algorithms (FedAvg, FedProx, FedAdam, SCAFFOLD, FedDyn) $\times$ 6 partitions (natural-by-BS $+$ Dirichlet $\alpha \in \{0.05..5.00\}$) $\times$ 10 seeds $=$ 900 cells, with NVML idle-baseline-subtracted training-energy measurement on RTX~4080 and controlled run-level vs row-level partitioning experiments on $4\times$ V100-SXM2-32GB that diagnose the apparent inverted-$\alpha$ as a sequence-integrity artifact. Four findings: (1) \textbf{an apparent ``inverted heterogeneity'' --- the natural base-station partition seemingly beating every Dirichlet $\alpha$ --- is a sequence-integrity artifact}: the de-facto FL non-IID partitioning (row-level Dirichlet/random assignment) scatters each run's consecutive timesteps across clients and corrupts the per-client sliding windows, while natural-by-BS happens to keep runs intact; a run-level Dirichlet control at matched $\alpha$ recovers full performance and erases the inversion (the per-client contiguous-window fraction rank-correlates perfectly with AUC), so the inversion is a benchmarking artifact, not a RAN structural property --- a previously-unflagged pitfall for federated time-series benchmarks, for which we give the diagnosis, a run-level fix, and a corrected benchmark; (2) server-side algorithmic gains are small --- FedAdam improves over FedAvg by at most $+0.016$ AUC in the Dirichlet sweep, and a 5-seed FedSWA probe at LSTM $\times$ natural-by-BS exceeds FedAdam by only $+0.0004$ AUC, suggesting no large LookAhead-style gain in this representative cell; (3) selective-state-space backbones interact catastrophically with control-variate algorithms at moderate skew (Mamba $\times$ SCAFFOLD $\times$ $\alpha \in \{0.10, 0.50\}$); (4) architecture choice traverses $10\times$ in commodity-GPU energy and an order of magnitude larger AUC range than algorithm choice. A five-architecture extension (adding xLSTM and Mamba-3) exhibits the same apparent advantage, as expected of an artifact that is architecture-agnostic by construction (corrupted sliding windows degrade any sequence backbone). We provide a reference benchmark with paired-bootstrap CI$_{95}$ over 270 paired distributions, NVML-instrumented per-cell energy, and a preregistered statistical protocol, released under AGPL-3.0 with Croissant metadata and archived at Zenodo (concept DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20263144).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

\IEEEPARstart{O}{pen} Radio Access Networks (O-RAN) decomposes the cellular base station into commodity hardware components controlled by ML-driven xApps and rApps running on disaggregated RAN Intelligent Controllers (RICs)~\cite{Polese2022}. Slice-level Service Level Agreement (SLA) violation forecasting is one near-RT RIC xApp pattern of practical interest: each gNB simultaneously serves heterogeneous traffic slices (the ColO-RAN release exercises eMBB constant-bitrate, MTC Poisson, and URLLC Poisson --- see Section~\ref{sec:dataset}), and proactive forecasting of next-second SLA risk informs near-RT RIC resource-allocation decisions within its 10\,ms\,--\,1\,s control loop. Because edge gNBs accumulate per-user telemetry whose central pooling is undesirable or infeasible (operator policy, regulatory privacy, backhaul bandwidth), federated learning (FL) is a natural training paradigm: each gNB trains a local sequence model on its own traces and shares only weight updates with the FL aggregator. We place the aggregator in the non-RT RIC / SMO orchestration layer ($\geq 1$\,s timescale fits our 100-round federated training cadence per Section~\ref{sec:method}); a near-RT-RIC-internal xApp aggregator would alternatively be possible if cross-cell xApp messaging supports the federation protocol --- we do not engage with that deployment alternative beyond noting it.

Standard FL practice --- codified by a decade of toy-image benchmarks --- treats client heterogeneity as a \emph{wound} to be healed: lower Dirichlet $\alpha$ (more skew) drives lower accuracy, motivating sharpness-aware methods, control variates, and proximal regularizers. \textbf{In O-RAN slice SLA prediction, this premise \emph{appears} to invert} --- for an instructive reason we unpack below. When clients are the \emph{natural} base stations of a Colosseum/ColO-RAN testbed (one client per gNB), the federated average across 7 base-station-natural clients consistently outperforms every uniform-Dirichlet partition of the same training data on the same model + algorithm. Empirically (Section~\ref{sec:results}, all values are out-of-sample test AUC averaged over 10 seeds), the natural-by-BS partition achieves AUC 0.913--0.918 on LSTM (5-algo range; $\Delta\approx0.005$), 0.8998--0.9186 on Mamba (with SCAFFOLD as the lower outlier and the other four algorithms in 0.911--0.919), and 0.840--0.856 on Spiking-SSM, \textbf{strictly above the AUC obtained at any parametric Dirichlet $\alpha$} for every (arch, algo) pair, and monotonically increasing as $\alpha \rightarrow 0$ within the Dirichlet sweep. The $\alpha=5.0 \rightarrow \alpha=0.05$ AUC gap is $\approx 0.10$--$0.12$ on the dense LSTM/Mamba backbones; on Spiking-SSM the gap is smaller ($\approx 0.022$) but still positive, and across all five algorithms heterogeneity is associated with higher AUC, not lower. We initially read this as evidence of cell-conditional structure preserved by bs grouping. Controlled run-level partitioning experiments (Section~\ref{sec:why-heterogeneity-helps}) overturn that reading: the collapse under Dirichlet or \texttt{random\_split} is not about losing bs grouping per se but about \textbf{row-level partitioning scattering each run's consecutive timesteps across clients}, which corrupts the per-client sliding windows the sequence models depend on. A run-level Dirichlet partition (intact runs, Dirichlet-skewed) at matched $\alpha$ recovers natural-by-BS AUC and erases the inversion; breaking bs-coherence while keeping runs intact (\texttt{run\_random}) costs ${\sim}0$ AUC (paired CI$_{95}$ $[-0.0003, +0.0001]$). The apparent inversion is therefore a \textbf{sequence-integrity artifact of how the non-IID partitions are constructed}, not a structural property of RAN telemetry. The deployment recommendation is unchanged and trivial --- partition by natural gNB, which preserves each cell's time series --- but the \emph{scientific} reading that RAN telemetry has special cell-conditional structure defeating standard FL heterogeneity does not survive the controls.

Despite the explosion of FL benchmarks since 2018, the deployment design space for FL-based slice SLA prediction remains under-characterized. The space spans four axes:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Sequence model architecture} --- recurrent (LSTM), structured state-space (Mamba), or bio-inspired sparse spiking variants (Spiking-SSM).
    \item \textbf{Federation algorithm} --- FedAvg, FedProx, FedAdam, SCAFFOLD, FedDyn (and the more recent sharpness-aware family discussed in Section~\ref{sec:related}).
    \item \textbf{Client heterogeneity} --- including the natural base-station partition (one gNB $\Rightarrow$ one client) and parametric Dirichlet stress $\alpha\in\{0.05, 0.10, 0.50, 1.00, 5.00\}$ over slice IDs.
    \item \textbf{Commodity hardware} --- consumer-tier edge GPUs (RTX-class) at the gNB rather than data-center accelerators.
\end{enumerate}

Existing benchmarks address each axis in isolation. NeurIPS-class FL benchmarks evaluate algorithm $\times$ heterogeneity on toy image data~\cite{Caldas2018_LEAF, Lai2022_FedScale, Song2022_FLAIR, Granqvist2024_pflresearch}. Spiking-NN benchmarks compare architectures in centralized regimes on neuromorphic-friendly datasets~\cite{Shen2025_STEP}. FL $\times$ wireless papers focus on a single architecture~\cite{FedLSTM2022, arXiv2508_08479, Mangi2026}. The implicit assumption underlying single-axis recommendations: \textbf{best architecture $\times$ best algorithm $\times$ representative $\alpha$ $\times$ commodity hardware $=$ best deployment}.

\textbf{This paper tests the assumption empirically.} We construct the first comprehensive 4-axis FL benchmark on the publicly-available Colosseum/ColO-RAN testbed dataset~\cite{Polese2022, Bonati2024}, sweeping \{LSTM, Mamba, Spiking-SSM\} $\times$ \{FedAvg, FedProx, FedAdam, SCAFFOLD, FedDyn\} $\times$ \{natural-by-BS, Dirichlet $\alpha\in\{0.05, 0.10, 0.50, 1.00, 5.00\}$\} $\times$ 10 seeds $=$ 900 cells (90 groups, all 10 seeds populated), with NVML-instrumented per-round GPU energy measurement on RTX~4080 (sm\_89) commodity hardware. Statistical inference uses paired-bootstrap CI$_{95}$ on per-seed AUC deltas; Wilcoxon $p$-values are reported uncorrected per finding (with family-wise Bonferroni discussed in Section~\ref{sec:stats} as a supplementary check).

\textbf{Contributions}:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{A sequence-integrity pitfall in federated time-series benchmarking (primary contribution)}: we first observe an apparent ``inverted heterogeneity'' --- the natural base-station partition outperforms every parametric Dirichlet $\alpha$ across all architectures and algorithms, contradicting the standard ``skew hurts'' assumption. Through controlled, same-environment, paired run-level partitioning experiments (\texttt{run\_random}; run-level Dirichlet) we show this is a \textbf{sequence-integrity artifact}: the standard row-level non-IID partitioning scatters each run's consecutive timesteps across clients, so per-client sliding-window construction yields temporally-broken windows; natural-by-BS merely keeps runs intact. With intact sequences AUC is flat across heterogeneity and coherence (run-level Dirichlet $\approx$ natural at every $\alpha$; breaking bs-coherence with intact runs costs ${\sim}0$ AUC, paired CI$_{95}$ $[-0.0003, +0.0001]$; shown for LSTM, confirmed on Mamba and Spiking-SSM in Section~\ref{sec:why-heterogeneity-helps}), while the row-level deficit and its $\alpha$-dependence track window-fragmentation severity. Because row-level Dirichlet partitioning is the de-facto FL-benchmark default (NIID-Bench) and is routinely transplanted to federated time-series tasks, this pitfall plausibly affects other federated time-series benchmarks; we provide the diagnosis, a sequence-preserving (run-level) partitioning fix and a checklist, and a corrected O-RAN slice-SLA benchmark.

    \item \textbf{Algorithmic leverage is small in this benchmark.} Among five preregistered FL algorithms, FedAdam is best but improves over FedAvg by at most $+0.016$ AUC (Dirichlet-averaged) in our sweep; per-architecture paired-bootstrap CI$_{95}$ details in Section~\ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling}. A 5-seed FedSWA probe on LSTM $\times$ natural-by-BS (Section~\ref{sec:why-no-fedswa}) changes the ranking by only $+0.0004$ AUC $[+0.0002, +0.0006]$, and FedBN~\cite{Li2021_FedBN} is structurally equivalent to FedAvg for our no-BatchNorm backbones (proof $+$ 30-cell empirical confirmation in Section~\ref{sec:limits} L2). A 60-cell extension with FedSCAM~\cite{FedSCAM2026} and FedGMT~\cite{FedGMT2025} (Section~\ref{sec:sam-family-empirical}) finds FedSCAM extracts a $+0.0092$ AUC paired gain at the moderate-heterogeneity $\alphadir = 0.10$ cell (5/5 same-direction, CI$_{95}$ excludes zero) but ties FedAvg at IID and extreme $\alphadir$, while FedGMT is strictly dominated by FedAdam across all six partitions tested ($\Delta \in [-0.027, -0.005]$). These results suggest that, in this small-$N$ high-participation regime, architecture and partition choices dominate server-side optimiser choice, with one moderate-heterogeneity niche for per-client SAM radius modulation. FedMoSWA~\cite{FedMoSWA2025} empirical evaluation on Colosseum/ColO-RAN remains deferred to follow-up work; the mechanism-based discussion is in Section~\ref{sec:why-fedadam-saturates}.

    \item \textbf{Architecture $\times$ algorithm catastrophic interaction (Mamba $\times$ SCAFFOLD $\times$ $\alpha\in\{0.10, 0.50\}$)}: SCAFFOLD on Mamba at $\alpha=0.10$ yields AUC $0.7609 \pm 0.0830$ --- std amplified $\sim 3\times$ over (Mamba, FedAvg) at the same $\alpha$ (0.0251) and $\sim 10\times$ over (Mamba, FedAvg) at $\alpha=5.0$ (0.0073). The deployment warning is concrete: control-variate methods interact pathologically with selective-scan SSMs under high heterogeneity, even as both components individually behave well on neighboring cells.

    \item \textbf{Architecture leverage dominates algorithm leverage} (paired-bootstrap CI$_{95}$) \textbf{on RTX~4080}: on the energy-AUC Pareto front (Section~\ref{sec:results}), architecture choice spans $10\times$ in NVML-measured model-attributable energy on RTX~4080 (LSTM $\sim 3.5$\,kJ $\rightarrow$ Mamba $\sim 10.5$\,kJ $\rightarrow$ Spiking $\sim 41$\,kJ per cell). Holding (algorithm, IID partition) fixed, the paired Spiking $-$ LSTM AUC delta is concentrated at $-0.061$ to $-0.074$ across all 5 algorithms (all 5 cells significant at $p<0.005$), substantively dwarfing the FedAdam $-$ FedAvg algorithmic gap of $+0.006$ to $+0.016$ on the same architectures (contribution 2). The $10\times$ span is hardware-specific and \textbf{implementation-specific}: on a sparsity-aware accelerator (Loihi-2, IBM NorthPole) the LSTM-vs-Spiking ratio would compress; and within our pure-PyTorch implementation, Mamba uses a sequential-scan kernel rather than the optimised \lstinline|mamba-ssm| Triton kernel, which would tighten the LSTM-vs-Mamba gap on this implementation but does not affect the LSTM-vs-Spiking band-separation conclusion (Section~\ref{sec:arch-leverage} + Section~\ref{sec:limits} L1, L6). Operator decisions on this RTX~4080 implementation should centre architecture-energy trade-off, not algorithm breadth.

    \item \textbf{Open reproducible reference benchmark}: spec-driven YAML pipeline with pre-flight algorithm validation and \lstinline|--skip-completed| resumability; paired-bootstrap statistical pipeline; NVML idle-baseline-subtracted training energy; Croissant metadata; Zenodo-archived (concept DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20263144); AGPL-3.0 license (preregistered). The full 4-axis table maps deployment configurations $\rightarrow$ (AUC, F1, energy, paired CI$_{95}$) over 90 (arch $\times$ algorithm $\times$ partition) groups, enabling lookup-style operator decisions on Colosseum/ColO-RAN.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Positioning against concurrent work.} Our contribution is empirical multi-architecture characterisation on RAN telemetry, not the proposal of a new federated algorithm or backbone. \textbf{FedRMamba}~\cite{FedRMamba2026} (WWW~2026, concurrent submission) is the closest contemporary method-proposal in the federated-Mamba time-series space; it differs from our work in contribution type (method-design vs cross-architecture benchmark) and domain (general multivariate forecasting vs RAN slice-SLA prediction) --- we discuss the differentiation in detail in Section~\ref{sec:related-concurrent}. We do not claim first-of-kind status for the individual federated Mamba / xLSTM / Mamba-3 instances; we claim the first comprehensive cross-architecture FL benchmark on the publicly-available Colosseum/ColO-RAN testbed with paired-bootstrap inference and per-round NVML energy. We additionally extend the 3-architecture core panel with xLSTM~\cite{Beck2024_xLSTM} and Mamba-3~\cite{Lahoti2026_Mamba3} as a robustness check against the most recent recurrent / state-space backbones; the 5-architecture panel is reported in Section~\ref{sec:path-d-ext}.

Section~\ref{sec:repro} enumerates the reproducibility infrastructure and our Croissant-aligned data release. Section~\ref{sec:results} reports results across the 90 (arch $\times$ algorithm $\times$ partition) groups with paired-bootstrap CI$_{95}$. Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes.


\section{Related Work}\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Federated learning benchmarks}

The canonical FL benchmark is \textbf{LEAF}~\cite{Caldas2018_LEAF}, which standardized federated MNIST/Shakespeare evaluation pipelines for early FedAvg variants. \textbf{Flower}~\cite{Beutel2020_Flower} later provided a framework rather than a benchmark, supporting heterogeneous device experiments. \textbf{FedScale}~\cite{Lai2022_FedScale} scaled to thousands of cross-device clients; \textbf{FLAIR}~\cite{Song2022_FLAIR} introduced a long-tail multi-label image dataset. \textbf{pfl-research}~\cite{Granqvist2024_pflresearch} integrates differential-privacy mechanisms into a simulation framework. Most recently, \textbf{fev-bench}~\cite{Shchur2025_fevbench} introduced rigorous bootstrap-CI$_{95}$ evaluation for time-series forecasting, though without the federated dimension. None of these benchmarks targets O-RAN slice SLA prediction specifically; their toy-data settings cannot capture the covariate-skew structure of real RAN telemetry.

\subsection{FL on cellular and wireless networks}

A first generation of FL $\times$ cellular work targeted SLA-constrained slicing under the Beyond-5G banner. \textbf{Statistical FL for B5G SLA-Constrained RAN Slicing}~\cite{Statistical2021_B5G} introduced a long-term CDF SLA constraint. \textbf{CDF-Aware FL}~\cite{CDFaware2021} formalized the constraint as a per-client penalty. \textbf{Fed-LSTM}~\cite{FedLSTM2022} deployed an LSTM forecaster for slice-level traffic. \textbf{TRACTOR}~\cite{Groen2023_TRACTOR} integrated reinforcement-learning xApps for resource-block assignment. \textbf{REAL}~\cite{Barker2025_REAL} integrated the OSC near-RT RIC with srsRAN for RL-based slice-resource-allocation xApps under urban-mobility channel modelling. Recently \textbf{Hayek et al.\ 2025}~\cite{Hayek2025} measured FL convergence on a 5G/WiFi/Ethernet COTS testbed with Raspberry-Pi clients, exposing uplink straggler effects. \textbf{arXiv:2508.08479}~\cite{arXiv2508_08479} benchmarked FedAvg/FedProx/FedBN on five throughput-prediction datasets with LSTM, CNN, CNN+LSTM, and Transformer architectures, finding FedBN superior under feature skew. \textbf{Mangi et al.\ 2026}~\cite{Mangi2026} (``\textsc{When Clients Drift}'') proposed regime-aware aggregation under leave-one-regime-out evaluation on synthetic 6G RAN telemetry.

Each of these papers covers one or two of our four axes. None benchmarks LSTM, Mamba, and Spiking-SSM jointly under parametric Dirichlet heterogeneity, and none reports per-round NVML energy. Our work is differentiated by the combination of architecture breadth, algorithm breadth, parametric heterogeneity, and energy instrumentation on the \textbf{publicly-available} Colosseum/ColO-RAN dataset, in contrast to closed simulators or undisclosed datasets used by Mangi et al.

\subsection{Spiking-SSM and FL $\times$ spiking neural networks}

Hybrid spiking-state-space architectures emerged from late 2024. \textbf{SpikMamba}~\cite{Chen2024_SpikMamba} combined LIF spiking neurons with Mamba selective scan for event-camera action recognition. \textbf{Mamba-Spike}~\cite{arXiv2408_11823_MambaSpike} introduced a spiking front-end for general temporal data. \textbf{Spiking Point Mamba}~\cite{SpikingPointMamba2025} extended to 3D point clouds. \textbf{SpikingSSMs}~\cite{SpikingSSMs2025} formalized sparse-parallel spiking state-space layers. \textbf{SpikingMamba}~\cite{arXiv2510_04595_SpikingMamba} distilled large language models into spiking variants for energy efficiency. \textbf{SpikingBrain}~\cite{arXiv2509_05276_SpikingBrain} scaled spiking architectures to 76B parameters on data-center GPU clusters with explicit FLOP-utilization measurement.

Federated-learning variants of spiking neural networks emerged earlier than spiking-SSM: \textbf{arXiv:2106.06579}~\cite{arXiv2106_06579_FLSNN} founded FL $\times$ SNN. \textbf{FedLEC}~\cite{arXiv2412_17305_FedLEC} extended to label-skew non-IID. \textbf{SNN in vertical FL}~\cite{arXiv2407_17672_VFL_SNN} examined VFL energy trade-offs. \textbf{Robustness of SNN in FL with compression}~\cite{arXiv2501_03306_RobustSNNFL} addressed Byzantine attacks. \textbf{Privacy in FL with SNN}~\cite{arXiv2511_21181_PrivSNNFL} characterized gradient leakage. Most recently, \textbf{arXiv:2602.12009}~\cite{arXiv2602_12009_DPSNNFL} examined firing-rate sensitivity to differential privacy in federated SNN --- preempting the DP-SNN-FL angle. We accordingly cite this work in Section~\ref{sec:discussion} as a deferral and do not duplicate the DP study; our contribution is orthogonal in its multi-architecture $\times$ parametric-Dirichlet $\times$ NVML scope.

To our knowledge, no published work combines spiking-SSM with FL on cellular/RAN telemetry. Our \lstinline|spiking_expand2| variant adapts the wide-inner-state design pattern from Mamba~\cite{GuDao2023_Mamba} to spiking-SSM and demonstrates competitive AUC/energy trade-off on commodity edge GPU under FL.

\subsection{GPU energy measurement methodology}\label{sec:related-energy}

The argument that FLOP counts mis-estimate actual hardware energy predates our work. \textbf{Shen et al.\ 2023}~\cite{Shen2023_BitBudget} (``Is Conventional SNN Really Efficient?'') critiqued synaptic-operation accounting via a Bit Budget framework, finding that quantized ANNs dominate SNNs at equivalent bit budgets. \textbf{$\alpha$-FLOPs}~\cite{Asperti2021_alphaFLOPs} proposed a parallelism-aware alternative. \textbf{NeuroBench}~\cite{Yik2025_NeuroBench} standardized neuromorphic benchmarking on real hardware. The \textbf{ML.ENERGY Benchmark}~\cite{Chung2025_MLEnergy} measured inference energy on generative AI; \textbf{Where Do the Joules Go?}~\cite{Chung2026_Joules} diagnosed the breakdown across model families. \textbf{STEP}~\cite{Shen2025_STEP} specifically benchmarked spiking transformers including memory-access cost. \textbf{Beyond Backpropagation}~\cite{Spyra2025_BeyondBackprop} applied NVML and CodeCarbon to alternative training objectives.

We follow the \textbf{Energy API} approach (\lstinline|nvmlDeviceGetTotalEnergyConsumption|, available on Volta+ with NVML $\geq 11.0$) preferred over polling-power Riemann-sum integration per the ML.ENERGY 2025 paper~\cite{Chung2025_MLEnergy} and accompanying leaderboard. Our novel angle is the \textbf{federated-training regime}: prior NVML benchmarks measure inference~\cite{Yik2025_NeuroBench, Chung2025_MLEnergy} or centralized training~\cite{arXiv2509_05276_SpikingBrain, Spyra2025_BeyondBackprop}; we measure per-round energy across an FL sweep, including idle-baseline subtraction.

\subsection{Heterogeneity in federated learning}

\textbf{NIID-Bench}~\cite{Li2022_NIIDBench} established Dirichlet partitioning as the standard non-IID benchmark. \textbf{FedBN}~\cite{Li2021_FedBN} keeps client-local BatchNorm statistics for feature-skew settings; \textbf{arXiv:2508.08479}~\cite{arXiv2508_08479} demonstrated FedBN's superiority on cellular feature-skew. \textbf{pFedFDA}~\cite{pFedFDA2024} specifically targets covariate shift. \textbf{Borazjani et al.\ 2025}~\cite{Borazjani2025} argued for embedding-based heterogeneity definitions beyond label-skew.

We use Dirichlet partitioning over slice IDs as the canonical covariate-skew model for O-RAN: each gNB serves a different mix of slice traffic, with mixing entropy controlled by \alphadir. We adopt $\alphadir\in\{0.05, 0.10, 0.50, 1.00, 5.00\}$ to span extreme stress (each gNB dominated by a single slice) through near-IID. We additionally evaluate the natural base-station partition (one gNB $\Rightarrow$ one client, 7 ColO-RAN gNBs in the public release) as a non-parametric heterogeneity reference. We discuss the applicability of embedding-based alternatives in Section~\ref{sec:discussion}.

\subsection{Sharpness-aware federated learning (2024--2026)}\label{sec:related-sharp}

A separate strand of recent FL work targets \emph{heterogeneity-induced sharp minima} via per-step learning-rate cycling and server-side weight extrapolation. \textbf{FedSAM}~\cite{Caldarola2022_FedSAM} adapted Foret et al.'s SAM perturbation to the federated client step. \textbf{FedSWA}~\cite{Liu2025_FedSWA} introduced an intra-round cyclical local LR schedule (eq.~3) combined with a LookAhead-style server EMA $\theta_t = \theta_{t-1} + \alpha(v_t - \theta_{t-1})$ with $\alpha=1.5$ (eq.~17). \textbf{FedSCAM}~\cite{FedSCAM2026} composed SAM with clustering for further sharpness regularization. The reported gains (FedSWA: $+5.6\%$ over FedAvg on CIFAR-100 with $10\%$ client participation across 1000 rounds) assume large-$N$ low-participation FL with coherent client drift toward sharp minima.

We do not include this family in the main 5-algorithm preregistered baseline; an empirical 5-seed paired test of FedSWA at LSTM $\times$ natural-by-BS is reported in Section~\ref{sec:why-no-fedswa}, and a 60-cell extension to FedSCAM + FedGMT~\cite{FedGMT2025} across IID + 5 Dirichlet alphas is reported in Section~\ref{sec:sam-family-empirical} (FedMoSWA remains deferred). Two structural mismatches make the original absence-from-baseline decision defensible on mechanism alone. First, our regime --- 7 base-station-natural clients with $71\%$ per-round participation, 100 rounds, RAN slice SLA prediction --- is \emph{aggregation-noise-dominated} rather than coherent-drift-dominated. Second, FedAdam's adaptive variance damping $\mathrm{Adam}(m, v)$ over $(v_t - \theta_{t-1})$ systematically dominates FedSWA's variance-blind LookAhead overshoot \emph{in expectation} in this regime: FedAdam knows when to step small (high $v$) and when to step normally (low $v$), while FedSWA's LookAhead extrapolation rate $\alphala$ (the FedSWA paper's $\alpha$-symbol; we write it $\alphala$ throughout to disambiguate from the Dirichlet $\alphadir$ of Section~\ref{sec:partition} and the FedDyn $\alphadyn$ of Section~\ref{sec:method}; FedSWA's preregistered value is $\alphala=1.5$) amplifies \emph{both} signal and noise uniformly. Empirically (Section~\ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling}), FedAdam already saturates the headroom available to server-side aggregation modifications at $+0.006$--$+0.016$ Dirichlet-averaged AUC over FedAvg across the 3 architectures (paired-bootstrap CI$_{95}$ in Section~\ref{sec:intro} contribution 2); LookAhead overshoot is unlikely to \emph{systematically} exceed this ceiling without the variance-estimation machinery FedAdam has and FedSWA lacks. We additionally note that FedSWA targets \emph{heterogeneity-as-sharpness-wound}, which our inverted-$\alpha$ finding (Section~\ref{sec:intro}, Section~\ref{sec:results}) indicates does not characterize this dataset.

\subsection{Concurrent {Mamba}-in-FL and recent recurrent / state-space work (2025--2026)}\label{sec:related-concurrent}

Our benchmark is contemporary to a growing body of federated state-space-model methodology, and we position our contribution explicitly against it. \textbf{FedRMamba}~\cite{FedRMamba2026} (WWW~2026, concurrent submission) proposes a personalised federated forecasting framework in which each client combines a global frequency-aware Mamba module with a local patch-wise Mamba module for multivariate time-series. Their contribution is \emph{method-proposal} for general multivariate forecasting; ours is \emph{multi-architecture empirical characterisation} on RAN telemetry with paired-bootstrap statistical inference --- the two contribution types are complementary rather than competing, and we do not duplicate their architectural innovation.

A separate strand concurrently advances individual recent architectures in wireless and cellular settings. Ali et al.~\cite{Ali2025_xLSTMCellular} (July~2025) apply the xLSTM sLSTM block to \emph{centralised} cellular traffic forecasting; we extend the same block to the federated O-RAN slice-SLA prediction setting in Section~\ref{sec:path-d-ext}. \textbf{WiMamba}~\cite{Raviv2026_WiMamba} (March~2026) positions Mamba as a wireless foundation model, supporting our state-space backbone inclusion. \textbf{SliceFed}~\cite{Mohammadi2026_SliceFed} (March~2026) targets federated O-RAN slicing via multi-agent DRL for spectrum-\emph{control} rather than supervised SLA \emph{prediction} --- adjacent scope, different task.

We therefore do \emph{not} claim first-of-kind status for the individual federated Mamba / xLSTM / Mamba-3 instances. Our novelty is the specific combination: comprehensive cross-architecture FL benchmark on the publicly-available Colosseum/ColO-RAN testbed with paired-bootstrap inference, per-round NVML idle-subtracted energy, and two contrarian empirical findings (inverted-$\alphadir$ heterogeneity and the Mamba\,$\times$\,SCAFFOLD\,$\times\alphadir{\in}\{0.10, 0.50\}$ catastrophic interaction; Sections~\ref{sec:inverted-alpha} and \ref{sec:mamba-scaffold}). A future paired-baseline 5-architecture extension (running xLSTM / Mamba-3 against FedAvg / FedAdam) is open work and is flagged in Section~\ref{sec:path-d-ext}.


\section{Dataset}\label{sec:dataset}

\subsection{Colosseum / ColO-RAN testbed}

ColO-RAN~\cite{Polese2022} is the publicly-released RAN telemetry corpus collected from the Colosseum digital-twin testbed~\cite{Bonati2024}, comprising the per-second downlink/uplink physical-layer measurements of 7 base stations (Colosseum Rome scenario, BS nodes $\{1, 8, 15, 22, 29, 36, 43\}$; we re-index these to \lstinline|bs_id| $\in\{1, \ldots, 7\}$ for ease of use) operating concurrently under 28 distinct traffic configurations (\lstinline|tr| $\in\{0, \ldots, 27\}$, each a different RBG-per-slice allocation pattern; the exact (slice~0 / slice~1 / slice~2) RBG triples per \lstinline|tr| are documented in the ColO-RAN release). Each base station serves 3 logical slices, and each slice can be served by one of 3 scheduling policies; both index mappings are listed in Table~\ref{tab:dataset-mapping}. The unified telemetry table contains $\approx 18$\,M rows of (timestamp, \lstinline|bs_id|, \lstinline|slice_id|, \lstinline|sched|, \lstinline|tr|) keyed observations with 17 continuous features (uplink/downlink throughput, MCS/CQI, BLER, RB utilisation, buffer occupancy; full list in Section~\ref{sec:method}). We use the public release~\cite{wineslab_coloran_repo} without modification; no synthetic augmentation, no re-simulation. The OOD train/val/test split by traffic-config index \lstinline|tr| (\lstinline|tr| $\in \{0..21\}$ train, \lstinline|tr| $\in \{22..24\}$ val, \lstinline|tr| $\in \{25..27\}$ test) is a leave-future-traffic-configs-out design, simulating deployment to unseen RBG allocation patterns.

\subsection{Task definition: per-slice SLA-violation forecasting}

The forecasting target is the binary indicator $y_{t+1} = \mathbf{1}[\mathit{ul\_bler}_{t+1} > 0.10]$, predicting whether the next-second uplink BLER on a given (\lstinline|bs_id|, \lstinline|slice_id|) tuple will breach the $10\%$ SLA threshold. This threshold is the canonical SLA-violation gate used by the ColO-RAN authors~\cite{Polese2022}; the binary framing keeps the metric (AUC, F1) decision-relevant for the near-RT RIC's resource-allocation xApps. The empirical positive rate on the train partition is \textbf{$30.9\%$} (per-slice $34.9\%$ / $26.7\%$ / $31.0\%$, measured on \lstinline|tr| $\in\{0..21\}$; see Section~\ref{sec:discussion} for how this rate enters the per-client loss reweighting). Inputs to the model are the most recent five seconds of (\lstinline|slice_id|, \lstinline|sched|, \lstinline|tr|, 17 continuous features) on the same (\lstinline|bs_id|, \lstinline|slice_id|) tuple --- \lstinline|seq_len=5| is preregistered from v3/v4.

\subsection{Out-of-distribution split by traffic config}

To prevent label leakage between train / validation / test, we partition the 28 traffic configurations into three disjoint groups: train \lstinline|tr| $\in\{0..21\}$ (22 configs), validation \lstinline|tr| $\in\{22..24\}$ (3 configs), and test \lstinline|tr| $\in\{25..27\}$ (3 configs). All three sets are evaluated on the same 7 base stations and 3 slices; only the traffic-config index changes. This split is identical to v4 so accuracy numbers are cross-paper comparable. Crucially, \textbf{partition-into-clients (Section~\ref{sec:partition}) operates within the train \lstinline|tr| range only}: validation and test sets are pooled across all clients to guarantee identical evaluation surfaces across configurations.

\subsection{Target-leakage audit (preserved from v4)}

A v4-era audit established that the previously documented \lstinline|allocation_efficiency = 0.5 * throughput_eff + 0.3 * qos + 0.2 * prb_util| regression target is a deterministic linear combination of three concurrent inputs and is therefore unsuitable. We retain that exclusion and the v4-validated leak-free continuous feature set in v7.

\subsection{Preprocessing pipeline}

The 18\,M-row unified parquet (\lstinline|coloran_raw_unified.parquet|) is materialised once and streamed lazily through four pipeline stages: (i)~per-(\lstinline|bs_id|, \lstinline|slice_id|) sequence construction with \lstinline|build_run_sequences| (rolling \lstinline|seq_len=5| windows, positive-rate computed from train rows only via \lstinline|pos_weight_split=train| (preregistered) --- this avoids test-set leakage into the loss function); (ii)~leak-free \lstinline|ContinuousScaler| fit on the train partition only and applied to validation and test; (iii)~categorical encoding of \lstinline|slice_id| $\times$ \lstinline|sched| $\times$ \lstinline|tr| via \lstinline|nn.Embedding| lookups; (iv)~Dirichlet-or-natural-by-BS client partition (Section~\ref{sec:partition}). All four stages are implemented as pure functions in \lstinline|src/fl_oran/data_v2/| and tested in \lstinline|tests/test_v5_*| for determinism and seed-reproducibility (single-source-of-truth contract). The same sequence index is shared across all algorithms within an (arch, partition, seed) triple via a \lstinline|SharedSplits| cache; this is the perf inheritance from prior centralized work.

\textbf{OOD note on the \lstinline|tr| embedding.} The \lstinline|tr| (traffic-config) categorical takes values $0..21$ in the train split and $25..27$ in the test split (no overlap, by construction of the OOD evaluation protocol). Test-time \lstinline|nn.Embedding(30, 8)| lookups for rows $22$--$29$ therefore return the original PyTorch $\mathcal{N}(0, 1)$ initialised vectors --- the model never observes test-\lstinline|tr| embeddings during training. This is a real OOD design weakness, but Section~\ref{sec:tr-embedding-confound} quantifies it: the natural-by-BS gap remains $90$--$98\%$ intact under a meanfix re-evaluation, and a no-\lstinline|tr| LSTM ablation slightly \emph{improves} over the with-\lstinline|tr| baseline (paired $\Delta = +0.0006$ AUC, CI$_{95}$ $[+0.0003, +0.0010]$). The structural lift therefore comes from the other categorical features (\lstinline|slice_id|, \lstinline|sched|) and the 17 continuous channel-state features, not from the \lstinline|tr| embedding.


\section{Method}\label{sec:method}

\subsection{Three architectures with a shared encoder--classifier shell}

All three architectures share an identical encoder and an identical classifier head; only the temporal backbone differs. The encoder concatenates \lstinline|nn.Embedding|-projected categorical features with the continuous features pre-standardised by the leak-free scaler (Section~\ref{sec:dataset}), producing a per-step input vector. The classifier head is a two-layer MLP \lstinline|Linear(b -> 64) -> ReLU -> Linear(64 -> 1)| consuming the backbone's last-step activation, where $b$ is the backbone's exposed dimension. Hyperparameter parity within $\pm 10\%$ of the LSTM baseline parameter count is preregistered and verified by \lstinline|tests/test_v6_param_count.py| (LSTM 44\,553, Mamba 40\,489, Spiking-expand2 43\,593). The negative direction of the Mamba parity gap ($-9\%$ relative to LSTM, vs Spiking-expand2's $-2\%$) reflects Mamba's structurally smaller state-space block at our preregistered configuration $(d_{\mathrm{model}}=64, d_{\mathrm{state}}=16, \mathit{expand}=1)$; we did not search over Mamba block dimensions to close this gap because (a)~the v6 architecture audit pinned these block dimensions and (b)~$\pm 10\%$ parity was the preregistered design constraint, not a hyperparameter optimisation outcome. The asymmetry should not be interpreted as compute-budget shortfall: at fixed \lstinline|seq_len=5| and \lstinline|max_steps=50|, gradient-step compute is dominated by the linear encoder/classifier shared across architectures, not by the backbone's parameter count.

\textbf{LSTM baseline (\lstinline|ForecasterV2|).} Two-layer stacked \lstinline|nn.LSTM| (input $\rightarrow$ 64 $\rightarrow$ 32). Unchanged since v3. Backbone exposes $b=32$.

\textbf{Mamba-S6 (\lstinline|MambaForecaster|).} \lstinline|Linear(input -> 64)| $\rightarrow$ $2 \times$ \lstinline|MambaS6Block| ($d_{\mathrm{model}}=64$, $d_{\mathrm{state}}=16$, $d_{\mathrm{conv}}=4$, $\mathit{expand}=1$) $\rightarrow$ \lstinline|Linear(64 -> 32)|. Each block performs (a)~input-dependent projection of $(\Delta t, B, C)$, (b)~depthwise causal 1-D convolution on the gating branch with SiLU non-linearity, (c)~sequential discretised SSM scan, and (d)~gated down-projection --- implementing the selective state-space block of Gu and Dao~\cite{GuDao2023_Mamba}. We use a pure-PyTorch sequential-scan implementation rather than the \lstinline|mamba-ssm| Triton kernel so the reproducibility artefact requires only a PyTorch + CUDA runtime, not a system CUDA-dev toolkit. Backbone exposes $b=32$.

\textbf{Spiking-SSM (\lstinline|spiking_expand2|).} \lstinline|Linear(input -> 56)| $\rightarrow$ $2 \times$ \lstinline|SpikingSSMBlock| ($d_{\mathrm{model}}=56$, $d_{\mathrm{state}}=16$, $\mathit{expand}=2 \Rightarrow d_{\mathrm{inner}}=112$, \lstinline|lif_threshold=1.0|, \lstinline|lif_beta=0.9|, \lstinline|atan_alpha=2.0|) $\rightarrow$ \lstinline|Linear(56 -> 32)|. Each block performs (a)~input projection, (b)~diagonal SSM recurrence with learnable $B, C, D$ matrices and log-parameterised $A$ initialised at $-[1, 2, \ldots, d_{\mathrm{state}}]$ per channel, (c)~per-channel \lstinline|snntorch.Leaky| LIF neuron emitting binary spikes via the atan surrogate gradient~\cite{Wei2018_atan}, (d)~\lstinline|out_proj| linear consuming the binary spike train. The $\mathit{expand}=2$ widening (vs $\mathit{expand}=1$ for the v6 default) is the architectural variant identified during prior wide-state experiments --- it dominates the alternative wide-Mamba (\lstinline|mamba_expand2|) variant on energy-AUC trade-off ($-11\%$ energy at parity AUC vs $+12\%$ theoretical / $+4\%$ measured energy at no AUC gain). Backbone exposes $b=32$.

\subsection{Five federated-learning algorithms}\label{sec:fl-algorithms}

We benchmark five algorithms drawn from the canonical preregistered set. In what follows we use $\alphadir$ for the Dirichlet partition concentration parameter (Section~\ref{sec:partition}) and $\alphadyn$ for the FedDyn regularisation coefficient, to disambiguate two distinct $\alpha$'s that appear in the FL literature with the same Greek letter.

\textbf{FedAvg}~\cite{McMahan2017_FedAvg} --- server-side aggregate is the per-client \lstinline|num_examples|-weighted average of client final states (\lstinline|scripts/aggregation.py:weighted_average_state_dicts|). In our spec each client trains for \lstinline|max_steps_per_round=50| $\times$ \lstinline|batch_size=64| $= 3200$ examples per round, so \lstinline|num_examples| is constant across clients and the weighted average reduces to a uniform mean --- but we mention the implementation detail explicitly because the released artefact aggregates by \lstinline|num_examples| and a different spec (varying \lstinline|max_steps| per client) would no longer give uniform weighting.

\textbf{FedProx}~\cite{Li2020_FedProx} --- FedAvg client step augmented with the canonical proximal regulariser, eq.~\eqref{eq:fedprox}, with $\mu = 0.01$.

\begin{equation}\label{eq:fedprox}
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{prox}}^{(i)} = \mathcal{L}^{(i)} + \frac{\mu}{2}\,\bigl\lVert w - w_g \bigr\rVert^2
\end{equation}

We inject the equivalent $\mu \cdot (w - w_g)$ correction directly into \lstinline|p.grad| after \lstinline|loss.backward()| (this is mathematically equivalent to adding the prox term to the loss but avoids building autograd nodes over every parameter).

\textbf{FedAdam}~\cite{Reddi2021_FedAdam} --- server-side Adam over the FedAvg pseudogradient $(v_t - \theta_{t-1})$ with first/second-moment coefficients $\beta_1 = 0.9$, $\beta_2 = 0.99$ (note: $\beta_2 = 0.99$ follows the FedAdam paper's choice rather than the canonical Adam default $0.999$) and server learning rate $\eta_{\mathrm{server}} = 0.01$.

\textbf{SCAFFOLD}~\cite{Karimireddy2020_SCAFFOLD} --- FedAvg client step augmented with a per-client control-variate correction $+(c_{\mathrm{global}} - c_{\mathrm{local},i})$ applied to gradients post-backward via the \lstinline|train_one_client_capped(grad_hook=...)| extension point introduced in our prior algorithm-interface design. For the per-client control-variate update we adopt the Adam-friendly Option-II variant as the default; eq.~\eqref{eq:scaffold} lists both options:

\begin{equation}\label{eq:scaffold}
  c_{\mathrm{local},i} \leftarrow
  \begin{cases}
    \nabla \mathcal{L}_i(w_g) & \text{(Option-II, default)} \\
    \dfrac{w_g - w_l}{K \, \eta} & \text{(Option-I, canonical SGD)}
  \end{cases}
\end{equation}

Option-I implicitly assumes SGD dynamics where weight drift scales with $\mathit{grad} \cdot \eta$; under our Adam local optimiser the drift is $\sim \eta$ regardless of gradient magnitude, making the Option-I $c_i$ estimate $\sim 100\times$ the true gradient magnitude and unstable across rounds.

\textbf{FedDyn}~\cite{Acar2021_FedDyn} --- we use the Adam-friendly Option-II variant as the default (with $\alphadyn = 0.01$); the canonical SGD-friendly variant diverges to NaN under our Adam local optimiser at the 100-round budget (root-cause analysis: positive-feedback between Adam's adaptive scaling and the unbounded $h$-accumulator growth). Both options are listed in eq.~\eqref{eq:feddyn}; the canonical variant is preserved as an opt-in \lstinline|update_mode="canonical"| for downstream researchers running SGD-only client optimisers.

\begin{equation}\label{eq:feddyn}
  h_i \leftarrow
  \begin{cases}
    h_i - \alphadyn \cdot \nabla \mathcal{L}_i(w_t) & \text{(Option-II, default)} \\
    h_i + (w_l - w_g) & \text{(canonical SGD)}
  \end{cases}
\end{equation}

MOON~\cite{Li2021_MOON} is deferred (preregistered before evaluation): its \lstinline|forecaster_encode_fn| is hard-bound to the LSTM backbone in v5 and the contrastive-feature extraction does not generalise cleanly to selective-scan SSM or to spiking-SSM activations; we leave the cross-architecture extension to future work and exclude MOON from the headline comparison rather than benchmark it on LSTM-only.

The five algorithms share a common \lstinline|FLAlgorithm| protocol with explicit \lstinline|init_state| / \lstinline|local_train| / \lstinline|server_aggregate| methods; each algorithm-specific contribution is injected as a closure over the shared \lstinline|train_one_client_capped| function, never as a duplicate training loop. The single deviation from the v5 $\rightarrow$ v7 design is one optional grad-hook parameter on \lstinline|train_one_client_capped| for the SCAFFOLD control-variate correction.

\subsection{Partition schemes: natural-by-BS and parametric Dirichlet}\label{sec:partition}

We evaluate two partition families holding 7 clients fixed.

\textbf{Natural-by-BS.} One client per ColO-RAN base station (\lstinline|bs_id| $\in\{1, \ldots, 7\}$). Each client receives its own gNB's full slice mix, scheduler distribution, and traffic-config samples (within the train \lstinline|tr| range). This is the partition that arises naturally in deployment, and is the central object of contribution~1 (Section~\ref{sec:intro}). \emph{Code-name caveat:} in our codebase this partition is implemented as \lstinline|partition_clients(mode="iid")|. The string \lstinline|"iid"| is a misnomer inherited from earlier development before we measured the per-bs distributions; the partition is \emph{not} statistically IID across clients on this dataset (per-bs continuous-feature distributions differ measurably --- see Section~\ref{sec:kl}). We keep the legacy \lstinline|mode="iid"| API name for backwards compatibility with the v5 / v6 codebase but refer to the partition as ``natural-by-BS'' throughout the paper. Researchers reproducing our pipeline should expect the file structure \lstinline|artifacts/v7_stage2_full/v7_<arch>_<algo>_iid_n7_s<seed>/| to correspond to natural-by-BS cells.

\textbf{Parametric Dirichlet.} For each value of $\alphadir \in \{0.05, 0.10, 0.50, 1.00, 5.00\}$, we draw per-client slice-id mixing proportions from $\mathrm{Dirichlet}([\alphadir, \alphadir, \alphadir])$ (3-dim, one component per \lstinline|slice_id| $\in\{0, 1, 2\}$) and assign train rows to clients according to those proportions. $\alphadir \rightarrow 0$ concentrates each client on a single dominant slice (extreme covariate skew); $\alphadir \rightarrow \infty$ approaches stratified uniform. This follows the canonical Hsu et al.\ construction~\cite{Hsu2019_Dirichlet} and is cross-comparable with NIID-Bench~\cite{Li2022_NIIDBench}. Implementation: \lstinline|src/fl_oran/data_v2/partition.py partition_clients(mode="dirichlet")|.

For every partition we use 7 clients, 100 rounds, 50 max steps per round, batch size 64, bf16 mixed precision, and \lstinline|cudnn_deterministic=True|. The per-round client sample is 5 of 7 ($71\%$ participation). Architecture-specific hyperparameter overrides (\lstinline|lr=5e-4| across all three; \lstinline|lr_warmup_rounds=5| for Spiking-SSM, 3 for the others) are preregistered in \lstinline|experiments/specs/stage2_full.yaml|.

\subsection{NVML training-energy measurement}

Per-round training energy is measured via \lstinline|nvmlDeviceGetTotalEnergyConsumption| (NVIDIA Energy API, available on Volta+ architectures) bracketed by \lstinline|torch.cuda.synchronize()| at round boundaries. We record both the raw cumulative energy and an \textbf{idle-baseline subtraction}: a 2-second NVML sample is taken before training to estimate the GPU's idle power draw $P_{\mathrm{idle}}$ (mW), and per-round training energy is reported per eq.~\eqref{eq:nvml}, yielding \lstinline|training_model_attributable_mJ| --- the model-attributable component used in Section~\ref{sec:results} and Figure~3 (Pareto).

\begin{equation}\label{eq:nvml}
  E_{\mathrm{round}} = E_{\mathrm{total}} - P_{\mathrm{idle}} \cdot \Delta t_{\mathrm{round}}
\end{equation}

This protocol follows the ML.ENERGY 2025 best-practices recommendation~\cite{Chung2025_MLEnergy} of preferring the cumulative Energy API over polling-power Riemann integration. Single-GPU only (multi-GPU not handled by design); \lstinline|nvidia-ml-py| is the canonical Python binding (the deprecated \lstinline|pynvml| package is used only as a fallback, with a deprecation warning logged at startup).

\subsection{Statistical inference}\label{sec:stats}

Statistical inference protocol (preregistered):

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Per-cell summary statistic.} Mean $\pm$ standard deviation of test AUC across the 10 seeds.
  \item \textbf{Pairwise comparison statistic.} Paired-bootstrap CI$_{95}$ on the per-seed AUC delta via \lstinline|scripts/aggregate_v7_results.py:paired_bootstrap_delta| ($n_{\mathrm{boot}} = 10\,000$, percentile interval). We choose percentile over BCa because $n = 10$ with approximately symmetric per-seed delta distributions makes the BCa bias-correction term smaller than the seed $\sigma$ on every reported finding (Section~\ref{sec:limits}).
  \item \textbf{Pairwise axis fix.} Each pairwise comparison holds all axes other than the contrasted one fixed (e.g., FedAdam-vs-FedAvg holds (arch, partition) constant; Spiking-vs-LSTM holds (algorithm, partition) constant).
  \item \textbf{Bootstrap RNG decorrelation.} Each pairwise comparison draws an independent BLAKE2b-derived seed offset added to the base bootstrap seed. Without this, every pair would resample at exactly the same indices, producing artificially correlated CIs and breaking any joint coverage claim across the 270 paired-bootstrap distributions.
  \item \textbf{Pair-set base seeds.} 2026 for the 180 algorithm-pair sweep; 2027 for the 90 architecture-pair sweep. The two sweeps' bootstrap streams are independent.
  \item \textbf{Secondary check.} Wilcoxon signed-rank $p$-value, as a directional sanity check; the paired-bootstrap CI is the primary statistic (preregistered).
  \item \textbf{Multi-comparison correction.} $p$-values reported uncorrected per finding. Family-wise Bonferroni at $\alpha = 0.05$ corresponds to threshold $0.05 / 180 \approx 2.78 \times 10^{-4}$ over the algorithm-pair family and $0.05 / 90 \approx 5.56 \times 10^{-4}$ over the arch-pair family. For the strictest joint coverage claim across the entire 270-pair set, the Mamba $\times$ SCAFFOLD finding (uncorrected Wilcoxon $p = 0.002$, see Section~\ref{sec:results}) sits above the corrected threshold, but its paired-bootstrap CI$_{95}$ excludes 0 with margin ($\Delta = -0.0915$, CI $[-0.1288, -0.0544]$). Readers seeking strict family-wise control should treat this Wilcoxon as supportive but not primary evidence.
\end{enumerate}

The five algorithms cluster within a $0.022$--$0.043$ test-AUC band on LSTM and Spiking-SSM (LSTM: FedAdam $0.813$ best, FedDyn $0.789$ worst; Spiking: FedAdam $0.696$ best, SCAFFOLD $0.653$ worst) and within a $0.078$ band on Mamba (FedAdam $0.816$ best, SCAFFOLD $0.738$ worst), with the wider Mamba spread driven by a single SCAFFOLD outlier. The within-architecture ordering is approximately stable: FedAdam $>$ FedAvg $\geq$ FedProx $>$ \{FedDyn, SCAFFOLD\} on every architecture, with the FedDyn/SCAFFOLD bottom-pair order architecture-dependent. By contrast, architectures are clearly separated on every partition: LSTM and Mamba sit in the $0.90$--$0.92$ IID band (with SCAFFOLD-on-Mamba as the $0.8998$ lower outlier and the four other algorithms in $0.911$--$0.919$) and $0.74$--$0.87$ Dirichlet stress band, while Spiking-SSM sits in $0.84$--$0.86$ IID and $0.65$--$0.71$ Dirichlet stress. The flat algorithm dimension is the design-space observation that motivates the Section~\ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling} algorithm-flatness analysis and the Section~\ref{sec:related-sharp} mechanism-based prediction (empirically tested in Section~\ref{sec:why-no-fedswa}) for the LookAhead-style sharpness-aware family.

\subsection{An apparent inverted-$\alpha$: natural BS grouping outperforms row-level Dirichlet partitions (diagnosed as an artifact in Section~\ref{sec:why-heterogeneity-helps})}\label{sec:inverted-alpha}

\begin{figure*}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{../artifacts/figures/interaction_heatmap.pdf}
\caption{Architecture $\times$ algorithm $\times$ partition interaction heatmap. Three rows (LSTM / Mamba / Spiking-SSM) $\times$ six partition columns (natural-by-BS $+$ Dirichlet $\alphadir \in \{0.05, 0.10, 0.50, 1.00, 5.00\}$). Each cell colour-codes mean test AUC over 10 seeds; cell annotation shows mean $\pm$ seed-std (n=10 seeds per cell). Figure~\ref{fig:interaction-heatmap} shows the empirical pattern that motivates the paper's central finding --- which Section~\ref{sec:why-heterogeneity-helps} diagnoses as a sequence-integrity artifact, not a structural property. For every (arch, algo) pair, AUC is monotonically increasing as Dirichlet $\alphadir$ decreases from $5.00$ toward $0.05$, and the natural-by-BS partition (one client per ColO-RAN gNB) sits \emph{strictly above} the most-skewed Dirichlet $\alphadir = 0.05$ cell on every (arch, algo). On LSTM $\times$ FedAvg, AUC traces $0.7475 \pm 0.0044$ ($\alphadir = 5.00$) $\rightarrow 0.7571$ ($\alphadir = 1.00$) $\rightarrow 0.7794$ ($\alphadir = 0.50$) $\rightarrow 0.8361$ ($\alphadir = 0.10$) $\rightarrow 0.8605 \pm 0.0161$ ($\alphadir = 0.05$) $\rightarrow 0.9159 \pm 0.0004$ (IID natural-by-BS), a $0.168$-AUC gap from the most-uniform Dirichlet to the natural partition. On Mamba $\times$ FedAvg the same trace is $0.749 \rightarrow 0.756 \rightarrow 0.782 \rightarrow 0.852 \rightarrow 0.869 \rightarrow 0.917$, and on Spiking $\times$ FedAvg $0.669 \rightarrow 0.669 \rightarrow 0.674 \rightarrow 0.679 \rightarrow 0.691 \rightarrow 0.853$. Paired LSTM$-$Mamba arch-deltas at IID are tight at $\Delta = -0.0006$ $[-0.0010, -0.0003]$ (FedAvg, $p = 0.002$), while at $\alphadir = 0.05$ they widen to $\Delta = -0.0081$ $[-0.0112, -0.0049]$, reflecting the same monotonicity at finer granularity. The pattern holds across every algorithm tested. Section~\ref{sec:why-heterogeneity-helps} shows this monotone $\alphadir$-curve is the signature of sequence corruption: row-level Dirichlet fragments each run's timesteps across clients, most severely at the most-uniform $\alphadir$ (every client receives a thin random slice of every run) and least at the most-skewed $\alphadir$ (a few clients retain most of each run). We measure this directly: the fraction of per-client sliding windows whose 5 rows have contiguous \lstinline|step_idx| falls monotonically from $1.00$ (natural-by-BS) through $0.84$ ($\alphadir{=}0.05$), $0.23$ ($0.10$), $0.15$ ($0.50$), to $0.004$ ($1.00$) and $0.001$ ($5.00$), and rank-correlates perfectly (Spearman $\rho{=}1$) with the AUC trace above. The standard ``lower $\alphadir \Rightarrow$ harder'' assumption is thus not refuted so much as confounded here --- the Dirichlet $\alphadir$-axis is entangled with per-client window corruption, and a sequence-preserving (run-level) Dirichlet at matched $\alphadir$ removes the effect entirely (Section~\ref{sec:why-heterogeneity-helps}).

\subsection{Algorithmic flatness: FedAdam saturates the server-side adaptive headroom}\label{sec:fedadam-ceiling}

The 180 paired-bootstrap algorithm-pair deltas confirm the visual flatness of Figure~\ref{fig:algo-ranking}. FedAdam consistently leads FedAvg in expectation. Per-architecture Dirichlet-averaged $\Delta_{\mathrm{FedAdam} - \mathrm{FedAvg}}$: LSTM $+0.0080$ (range $+0.0048$--$+0.0128$, all 5 cells significant at $p \leq 0.04$), Mamba $+0.0062$ (range $-0.0021$--$+0.0097$, 4 of 5 cells significant; $\alphadir = 0.05$ is the single non-significant cell at $p = 0.85$), Spiking $+0.0164$ (range $+0.0108$--$+0.0267$, 4 of 5 cells significant; $\alphadir = 0.05$ marginal at $p = 0.11$). At IID, $\Delta_{\mathrm{FedAdam} - \mathrm{FedAvg}}$ shrinks to $\approx +0.002$ across architectures (LSTM $+0.0019$ $[+0.0017, +0.0021]$, etc.) --- server-side adaptivity contributes most under heterogeneity stress and least where the federated average is already noise-quiet. The remaining four pairwise contrasts (FedProx, FedDyn, SCAFFOLD vs FedAvg) are bounded above by $\Delta_{\mathrm{FedAdam} - \mathrm{FedAvg}}$ in absolute magnitude on every (arch, partition) cell except for the catastrophic interactions discussed in Section~\ref{sec:mamba-scaffold}. This empirical ceiling --- $\approx +0.016$ AUC headroom for any server-side aggregation modification in our 5-algorithm preregistered set --- is the pivotal evidence supporting the Section~\ref{sec:related-sharp} mechanism-based prediction that LookAhead-style overshoot would not exceed FedAdam by more than this magnitude. Section~\ref{sec:why-no-fedswa} reports an empirical 5-seed paired test of this prediction at LSTM $\times$ natural-by-BS: FedSWA marginally exceeds FedAdam by $+0.0004$ AUC, well within the $+0.016$ envelope. The Section~\ref{sec:related-sharp} prediction is therefore split: the \textbf{magnitude prediction survived} (FedSWA$-$FedAdam stays inside the FedAdam-vs-FedAvg envelope), but the \textbf{directional ranking did not} (FedSWA $>$ FedAdam, opposite to the predicted FedSWA $<$ FedAdam). We do not claim the ceiling is ``validated'' since the test covers only one (LSTM, natural-by-BS) cell of one of three sharpness-aware methods.

\subsection{Architecture $\times$ algorithm catastrophic interaction: Mamba $\times$ SCAFFOLD}\label{sec:mamba-scaffold}

The flat-algorithm observation does not generalise across all (arch, algo) combinations. SCAFFOLD on Mamba at $\alphadir \in \{0.10, 0.50\}$ produces a destructive interaction not observed on LSTM, on Spiking-SSM, or on Mamba with any other algorithm at the same $\alphadir$. The headline cell --- Mamba $\times$ SCAFFOLD $\times$ $\alphadir = 0.10$ --- yields test AUC $0.7609 \pm 0.0830$ (mean $\pm$ std across 10 seeds), versus Mamba $\times$ FedAvg $\times$ $\alphadir = 0.10$ at $0.8524 \pm 0.0251$ on the same seeds. The paired delta is $\Delta_{\mathrm{SCAFFOLD} - \mathrm{FedAvg}} = -0.0915$ $[-0.1288, -0.0544]$ ($p = 0.002$, $n = 10$). The neighbouring cell at $\alphadir = 0.50$ reproduces the failure: $0.6955 \pm 0.0450$ vs $0.7816 \pm 0.0225$ for FedAvg, paired $\Delta = -0.0861$ $[-0.1026, -0.0689]$ ($p = 0.002$). Per-seed std is amplified $\approx 3\times$ over Mamba $\times$ FedAvg at the same $\alphadir$ ($0.083$ vs $0.025$) and $\approx 10\times$ over the most-uniform Dirichlet sweep (Mamba $\times$ FedAvg $\times$ $\alphadir = 5.00$, std $0.007$). Direction-consistency is total: \textbf{10 of 10 seeds have $\Delta < 0$ at $\alphadir = 0.10$} (per-seed deltas range $-0.0133$ to $-0.1689$) \textbf{and 10 of 10 seeds have $\Delta < 0$ at $\alphadir = 0.50$} --- the catastrophic interaction is reproducible across every seed, not driven by an outlier. Both components individually behave well: SCAFFOLD on LSTM $\alphadir = 0.10$ yields $0.8220 \pm 0.0362$ (no destruction), and Mamba on every other algorithm at $\alphadir = 0.10$ stays in $0.83$--$0.86$. The interaction is specific to the SCAFFOLD control-variate dynamics interacting with Mamba's selective-scan SSM under high partition skew, and is the single concrete deployment warning in this benchmark.

\begin{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Federation cost at matched compute budget.} Centralized LSTM at the \emph{same 25k gradient-step budget as FL} (5 seeds $\times$ 25\,000 steps each, \lstinline|experiments/specs/r2_same_step_centralized.yaml|) reaches \textbf{$0.9243$ $[0.9235, 0.9250]$} test AUC; FL at 100 rounds $\times$ 50 max-steps $\times$ 5 sampled clients $= 25$k gradient steps ($\approx 0.1$ centralized epoch) reaches \textbf{$0.9159$ $[0.9156, 0.9162]$}. \end{itemize}

\subsection{Mechanism preview + per-BS Dirichlet ablation evidence}\label{sec:mechanism-preview}

The four findings above raise one mechanistic question: \emph{why does heterogeneity \textbf{appear} to help here when standard FL benchmarks find the opposite?} Section~\ref{sec:discussion} resolves it. Briefly: two early candidates (per-client class-imbalance variance --- eliminated because \lstinline|fl_v7.py| uses a globally pooled BCE positive weight and the $30.9\%$ positive rate is balanced; and a bs$\leftrightarrow$slice mixture correlation --- eliminated by the per-bs slice-mixture KL $\approx 0$) were ruled out by direct measurement, and the run-level partitioning controls (Section~\ref{sec:run-level}) eliminate the remaining bs-grouping and heterogeneity candidates. The apparent inversion is a \textbf{sequence-integrity artifact}: row-level Dirichlet / \lstinline|random_split| scatter each run's timesteps and corrupt the per-client sliding windows, while natural-by-BS keeps runs intact. With intact sequences (run-level partitions) AUC is flat across $\alphadir$ --- heterogeneity per se does not help --- and the row-level deficit tracks window-fragmentation severity.

\textbf{Findings.} Three observations stand out:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{xLSTM has the highest absolute test AUC in 16 of 18 (algorithm, partition) cells.} The two exceptions occur at FedGMT $\times\alphadir{=}0.05$ (Mamba-3 leads by $+0.0062$) and FedGMT $\times\alphadir{=}0.10$ (Mamba leads by $+0.0098$). Elsewhere the xLSTM lead is typically $+0.01$ to $+0.02$ AUC over the next-best architecture. We report absolute means without claiming pairwise statistical significance --- a paired-seed cross-architecture bootstrap CI$_{95}$ tightening is open future work.
\item \emph{Mamba-3 tracks classical Mamba closely (max absolute deviation $0.011$ AUC over the 18 cells).} The ICLR~2026 inference-first redesign (improved discretisation $+$ complex state) does not visibly translate into a federated-supervised SLA-prediction advantage at our scale (${\sim}$40\,K parameters, 100 federated rounds, $\mathrm{seq\_len}{=}5$); both backbones occupy the same heterogeneity-response band.
\item \emph{Spiking-SSM is uniformly the lowest-AUC architecture in this SAM-family panel,} sitting $0.06$--$0.09$ AUC below the other four backbones at the IID anchor across all three algorithms. This echoes Sections~\ref{sec:arch-leverage} and \ref{sec:mamba-scaffold}: the surrogate-gradient spiking dynamics interact poorly with several FL local-perturbation regimes, while the energy--AUC trade-off characterised in Section~\ref{sec:arch-leverage-discussion} remains its main deployment-relevant property.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Determinism caveat.} As noted in Table~\ref{tab:five-arch}'s footnote, one cell (Mamba-3\,$\times$\,FedMoSWA\,$\times\alphadir{=}5.00$, seed $s{=}4$) terminated with \texttt{NonFiniteLossError} at round~94 step~14/50 of local SGD. A single deterministic retry under identical hyperparameters reproduced the failure \emph{bit-precisely} at the same round and step, confirming a deterministic (architecture\,$\times$\,optimiser\,$\times$\,partition) numerical-stability boundary rather than a stochastic NaN. We report $n{=}9$ paired seeds for that cell; the verified-deterministic reproduction also serves as an empirical confirmation of the cudnn-deterministic reproducibility setting used throughout Phase~5 and Path~D (Section~\ref{sec:repro}). The mechanism is consistent with Mamba-3's data-dependent complex rotation~$\theta_t$ entering an ill-conditioned regime under FedMoSWA's momentum-based weight averaging at low-heterogeneity Dirichlet $\alphadir{=}5.00$; we flag this as a numerical-stability caveat for the SSM\,$\times$\,momentum-averaged-FL combination, deferring a controlled investigation to future work.

\textbf{Implication for the §6 conclusions.} The 3-architecture core findings (LSTM as the latency--AUC sweet spot, Mamba as the efficiency--accuracy mid-point, Spiking-SSM as the energy-Pareto champion) are \emph{extended rather than overturned} by the 5-architecture panel: xLSTM emerges as a modest absolute-AUC leader under all three SAM-family algorithms, while Mamba-3's recent inference-first refinements do not visibly displace classical Mamba at the federated O-RAN scale studied here. We position this as a robustness check rather than a re-ranking; a paired-baseline 5-architecture comparison (running xLSTM and Mamba-3 against FedAvg / FedAdam to enable cross-architecture paired $\Delta$ analysis) is open future work.


\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Why natural BS grouping wins: empirical observation + tested-hypothesis structure}\label{sec:why-heterogeneity-helps}

The Section~\ref{sec:inverted-alpha} inversion of the Dirichlet-$\alphadir$ / AUC monotonicity is the paper's central empirical observation, and we now identify its mechanism. It is \textbf{fully explained by a sequence-integrity confound}: the row-level Dirichlet and \lstinline|random_split| partitions scatter each run's consecutive timesteps across clients, so the per-client \lstinline|build_run_sequences| step builds temporally-broken sliding windows, while natural-by-BS keeps each gNB's runs intact. We establish this with controlled run-level partitioning experiments (Section~\ref{sec:run-level}) that --- unlike the \lstinline|random_split| ablation (Section~\ref{sec:ablation}), which breaks bs-coherence and sequence integrity at once --- separate the two axes and show only sequence integrity matters.

\textbf{Setup-level facts (measured, not derived).} The training pipeline uses a \emph{globally pooled} positive-class weight in the BCE loss (\lstinline|fl_v7.py| line 636--637 sums positive counts across all client shards before computing the reweighting), so the BCE loss is identical across clients and any mechanism story relying on per-client class-imbalance variance is incompatible with the code. The empirical positive rate on the train partition is $30.9\%$ (per-slice $35\%$ / $27\%$ / $31\%$), so this is a balanced-ish binary task, not a sparse-positive-class regime. The dataset's per-bs slice mixture is statistically uniform (KL $\approx 0$; fitted Dirichlet $\hat{\alphadir} \approx 234\,000$), so natural-by-BS is \emph{not} a low-$\alphadir$ Dirichlet limit case --- the two partitions are orthogonal in their structural axis (\lstinline|bs_id| grouping vs \lstinline|slice_id| redistribution).

\textbf{Candidate explanations, and their elimination.} We initially entertained three candidates: (i)~per-bs \emph{continuous}-feature distributions differ across base stations (per-feature pairwise KL up to $0.08$; Section~\ref{sec:kl}); (ii)~the Dirichlet partition destroys bs-level grouping that natural-by-BS preserves; (iii)~a generic heterogeneity-as-implicit-regularisation effect. The run-level controls of Section~\ref{sec:run-level} \textbf{eliminate all three} and identify a fourth, decisive axis --- \textbf{sequence integrity}. Candidates~(ii) and~(iii) predict that breaking bs-coherence or adding skew should move AUC; instead \lstinline|run_random| (intact runs, bs-shuffled) ties natural-by-BS to within paired CI$_{95}$ $[-0.0003, +0.0001]$, and run-level Dirichlet (intact runs, skewed) holds AUC flat at natural across $\alphadir$. The only axis the run-level controls preserve and the row-level partitions destroy is the temporal contiguity of each run's sliding windows.

\subsubsection{Controlled ablation: random-split partition}\label{sec:ablation}

To isolate hypothesis~(ii) (bs-grouping preservation), we add a \textbf{\lstinline|random_split|} partition mode that uniformly shuffles all training rows and assigns equal-size shards to 7 clients, breaking \emph{both} \lstinline|bs_id| and \lstinline|slice_id| grouping. Per-client sample sizes are within $\pm 1$ row of \lstinline|len(train) / 7|, removing per-client sample-size confounds. Implementation: \lstinline|partition_clients(mode="random_split", n_clients=7, seed=...)| in \lstinline|data_v2/partition.py|.

The run-level controls (Section~\ref{sec:run-level}) hold sequence integrity fixed and show bs-coherence carries ${\sim}0$ of the effect; Section~\ref{sec:per-bs-ablation}'s per-BS Dirichlet (which scatters rows \emph{within} each BS) and Section~\ref{sec:kl}'s feature analysis are re-read in that light below.

\subsubsection{Run-level controls: the inversion is sequence corruption, not bs grouping}\label{sec:run-level}

\lstinline|random_split| breaks bs-coherence \emph{and} sequence integrity at once, so it cannot tell which axis carries the drop. We add two run-level partition modes that hold sequence integrity \emph{fixed} (\lstinline|run_random| and \lstinline|run_dirichlet| in \lstinline|data_v2/partition.py|: both assign whole \lstinline|(run_id, slice_id)| groups to clients, so every per-client window is over contiguous timesteps), varying only bs-coherence and skew. All cells are same-environment (V100, eager; fp32 for Mamba/Spiking, fp16 for LSTM; identical hyperparameters), and the LSTM contrasts are seed-paired with the same-environment natural-by-BS cells.

\textbf{\lstinline|run_random| (intact runs, bs-shuffled).} Whole runs assigned to 7 clients at random --- intact sequences, a random bs mix per client. LSTM $\times$ FedAvg, 5 seeds: mean test AUC $0.91583$ vs same-environment natural-by-BS $0.91572$ (paired $\Delta_{\mathrm{natural}-\mathrm{run\_random}} = -0.00011$, CI$_{95}$ $[-0.00030, +0.00013]$, straddles zero), while \lstinline|random_split| sits $0.176$ below. \textbf{Breaking bs-coherence with sequences intact costs essentially nothing}; the entire ${\sim}0.18$ \lstinline|random_split| deficit is the row-shuffle, eliminating candidates~(i) and~(ii).

\subsubsection{Per-bs continuous-feature distribution differences (Step 2 measurement)}\label{sec:kl}

We measured pairwise per-bs Kullback--Leibler divergence on every continuous feature (\lstinline|scripts/step2_mechanism_search.py|, output \lstinline|artifacts/step2_mechanism_search.json|). The top-5 most-discriminating features by mean pairwise bs-KL are \lstinline|dl_cqi| (KL $= 0.475$), \lstinline|dl_mcs| ($0.267$), \lstinline|num_ues| ($0.092$), \lstinline|tx_brate_dl_Mbps| ($0.081$), and \lstinline|sum_granted_prbs| ($0.059$). The top two --- channel-quality indicator (CQI) and modulation-and-coding scheme (MCS) --- are physical-layer features that depend on each gNB's specific RF environment and user-cell distance distribution, and their per-bs distributions differ by an order of magnitude more than the next-most-discriminating features. The remaining continuous features (BLER, RSSI, SINR, buffer occupancy) all have mean pairwise bs-KL below $0.02$, confirming that the bs-discriminative signal is concentrated in channel-state and load features rather than in BLER itself. These per-bs feature differences are real, but the run-level controls (Section~\ref{sec:run-level}) show they are \emph{not} the causal mechanism: \lstinline|run_random| spreads exactly these per-bs continuous features across clients (breaking bs-coherence) yet matches natural-by-BS to within paired CI$_{95}$ $[-0.0003, +0.0001]$, so the channel-state distributional signal is not what the natural partition exploits. The operative axis is the temporal contiguity of each run's sliding windows (sequence integrity; Section~\ref{sec:run-level}), which natural-by-BS preserves and every row-level partition (Dirichlet / \lstinline|random_split|) destroys.

\subsubsection{Applicability boundary}

The inverted-$\alphadir$ finding is dataset-structural, not algorithm-mechanistic; the conditions under which it should replicate to other near-RT RIC forecasting workloads are detailed in App.~A.1.

\subsubsection{Hardware drift caveat for the random-split ablation}\label{sec:hardware-drift}

Section~\ref{sec:ablation} cells ran on $4 \times$ Tesla V100-SXM2-32GB while Phase~5 ran on RTX~4080. We bound the cross-hardware AUC drift by comparing V100 \lstinline|random_split| AUC to 4080 Dirichlet $\alphadir = 5.00$ AUC (both partition strategies destroy bs grouping): the residual delta is at most $0.0072$ (LSTM), $0.0043$ (Mamba), $0.0021$ (Spiking-SSM), all within seed $\sigma$. Hardware drift is therefore bounded above by $\sim 0.007$ AUC, more than an order of magnitude smaller than the $\sim 0.18$ mechanism signal (ratio $\approx 25\times$). Full bf16-tensor-core argument and protocol detail in App.~A.2.

At $\alphadir = 0.05$ sub-clients are slice-specialists; at $\alphadir = 10$ each has near-uniform slice mixture. Headline numbers in Table~\ref{tab:per-bs-dirichlet}.

\subsubsection{Quantifying the tr-embedding-bug confound}\label{sec:tr-embedding-confound}

A code-level audit identified that the test traffic-config index \lstinline|tr| $\in \{25, 26, 27\}$ is encoded via \lstinline|nn.Embedding(30, 8)|, but the train set only ever indexes rows $\{0..21\}$. \subsection{Algorithmic flatness implies FedAdam is the ceiling, not the start}\label{sec:why-fedadam-saturates}

Section~\ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling} establishes that on this dataset the FedAdam-vs-FedAvg gap (Dirichlet-averaged $\approx +0.006$ to $+0.016$ AUC depending on architecture) bounds the empirical maximum gap that the 5 server-side aggregation algorithms in our preregistered baseline extract under our (7-client, $71\%$ participation, 100-round) regime. The Section~\ref{sec:why-no-fedswa} FedSWA add-on test extends this empirical ceiling by $+0.000379$ AUC at LSTM $\times$ natural-by-BS --- well within the $+0.016$ envelope, so the bound holds empirically even though FedSWA marginally exceeds FedAdam. The flatness is \emph{not} a property of FL in general --- large-$N$ low-participation FL on image classification regularly shows $\geq 0.05$ AUC gaps between FedAvg and FedSAM/FedSWA-class methods. The flatness is a property of \emph{aggregation-noise-dominated} small-$N$ high-participation regimes where the per-round update $(v_t - \theta_{t-1})$ reflects SGD noise across nearly-overlapping client averages rather than coherent drift toward sharp minima.

This explains the negative result on FedDyn-canonical: the canonical Acar 2021 SGD-friendly variant $h_i \leftarrow h_i + (w_l - w_g)$ diverges to NaN under our Adam local optimiser at the 100-round budget, due to a positive-feedback loop between Adam's adaptive scaling and the unbounded $h$-accumulation. Our Adam-friendly Option-II variant ($h_i \leftarrow h_i - \alphadyn \cdot \nabla \mathcal{L}_i(w_t)$ with $\alphadyn = 0.01$) reaches centralised parity (test AUC $0.9147$ on LSTM IID at 100 rounds, matching the centralised reference). The lesson generalises: server-side aggregation algorithms that perform well in their original SGD regime can require non-trivial recalibration under Adam local optimisers, and the absence of such recalibration is a frequent silent failure mode in the FL benchmark literature.

\subsection{The Mamba $\times$ SCAFFOLD $\times$ $\alphadir = 0.10$ catastrophic interaction}

The Section~\ref{sec:mamba-scaffold} finding warrants an explicit mechanism account. SCAFFOLD's per-client control variate $c_{\mathrm{local},i}$ is a running estimate of the client's average gradient direction relative to the global average, and the gradient correction $+(c_{\mathrm{global}} - c_{\mathrm{local},i})$ rotates each local update toward an ``unbiased'' trajectory in expectation. Under high partition skew ($\alphadir = 0.10$) the per-client gradient direction is itself highly variable across rounds (each client trains on a small, sparse-positive subset of the train rows), so $c_{\mathrm{local},i}$ is a noisy estimate. The selective-scan SSM in Mamba additionally has an input-dependent dynamics matrix ($\Delta t, B, C$ are all functions of the input --- Gu and Dao~\cite{GuDao2023_Mamba}), making the per-client gradient direction \emph{also} depend on the local partition's input distribution in a way the LSTM's input-independent recurrence does not. The combination --- noisy $c_{\mathrm{local},i}$ $\times$ input-dependent backbone dynamics --- produces a feedback loop where the SCAFFOLD correction overshoots in the direction of the prior-round's noise, and the next round's local training amplifies the overshoot rather than correcting it.

Mamba $\times$ FedAvg at the same $\alphadir$ does not exhibit this failure (std $0.025$ at $\alphadir = 0.10$ vs $0.083$ under SCAFFOLD), nor does LSTM $\times$ SCAFFOLD (std $0.036$ at $\alphadir = 0.10$) where the input-independent recurrence breaks the feedback. The pattern is therefore specific to the \emph{intersection} of input-dependent recurrence and noisy control-variate estimation; it does not generalise to all (state-space-model, control-variate) pairs but should be re-checked whenever SCAFFOLD or its variants are deployed alongside selective state-space architectures (S4, S5, S6, Mamba-2 --- the input-dependent SSM family generally) under partition skew. The SCAFFOLD hyperparameter envelope (client-LR, control-variate decay) was not exhaustively swept; see Section~\ref{sec:limits} for the limitation.

\subsection{Architecture leverage dominates algorithm leverage on commodity edge GPU}\label{sec:arch-leverage-discussion}

The Section~\ref{sec:arch-leverage} Pareto observation has direct deployment implications. On RTX~4080 (sm\_89, TSMC 4N (5\,nm class) Lovelace process, 320\,W TGP), the $10\times$ energy band between LSTM ($\sim 3.5$\,kJ/cell) and Spiking-SSM ($\sim 41$\,kJ/cell) is dominated by the spiking arm's $T = 5$ simulation timesteps multiplied through the dense post-spike linear layers --- the so-called spike-count amplification documented in our prior centralized findings. On a sparsity-aware accelerator (Loihi-2, IBM NorthPole) where post-spike multiplications by zero are skipped, the same architecture would sit closer to the LSTM band; the energy-Pareto disposition between LSTM and Spiking is therefore a property of the deployment hardware, not of the algorithm choice. We discuss the cross-hardware extrapolation in Section~\ref{sec:limits}.

The within-architecture algorithm spread ($\leq 0.03$ AUC at flat energy) is small enough that a deployed system should select algorithm based on operational considerations (state size --- FedDyn and SCAFFOLD carry per-client $h$ or $c$ variates; communication round count; client straggler tolerance) rather than chase the $+0.01$ AUC gain that FedAdam offers over FedAvg. The Section~\ref{sec:mamba-scaffold} catastrophic-interaction caveat is the principal exception: deployments combining selective-SSM backbones with control-variate algorithms should validate on representative high-skew partitions before committing to the combination.

\textbf{Honest caveats.} (i) Both algorithms run under Adam ($\eta = 5\mathrm{e}{-}4$) in our v7 pipeline; the paper versions of FedSCAM and FedGMT both use SGD with momentum $0.9$. (iii) Our FedGMT impl follows the reference \verb|harrylee999/FL-SAM| repository for the EMA-update timing (server round end), which differs by a one-round phase shift from paper Algorithm~1 line~7 (per-client round start). The two share the same recurrence $e_t = \alpha e_{t-1} + (1-\alpha) w_t$. (iv) Wilcoxon signed-rank $p$-values at $n = 5$ paired seeds floor at $0.0625$ (the most extreme outcome of 5/5 same-direction); the CI$_{95}$ exclusion of zero is therefore primary evidence here, and a follow-up $n = 10$ paired re-run on the cells flagged with $\dagger$ in Table~\ref{tab:sam-family-paired} is open future work. (v) The 4060~Ti KL-ablation used \verb|sample_ratio=0.1| (host-RAM constraint at the 4060~Ti's $30$\,GB vs V100's $754$\,GB); the cross-arm pairing on the same data slice ensures the $\Delta_{\mathrm{KL}}$ measurement is internally valid, but absolute AUCs ($\sim 0.66$ at IID) are not comparable to the V100 sweep's full-data values ($\sim 0.91$).

\subsection{Recommended practice: a partitioning checklist for federated time-series benchmarks}\label{sec:checklist}

The sequence-integrity artifact is not specific to our pipeline: it arises whenever a federated benchmark \emph{synthesises} client heterogeneity by row-level partitioning (Dirichlet over samples, or uniform random assignment --- the de-facto standard since NIID-Bench) of a \emph{pooled} time series and then constructs sliding windows per client. The row-level split scatters each entity's consecutive timesteps across clients, so per-client windows span temporal gaps; any sequence model degrades, and a sequence-preserving partition then looks spuriously superior. We distil a short checklist for FL-time-series benchmarking:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Partition by entity/sequence, not by row.} Assign whole entities (or whole \lstinline|(run, slice)| series) to clients; never shuffle rows before windowing.
  \item \textbf{Verify window contiguity.} Report the fraction of per-client sliding windows whose timesteps are contiguous; it should be $1.0$ (cf.\ the $0.001$--$0.84$ values for row-level Dirichlet, Section~\ref{sec:inverted-alpha}).
  \item \textbf{Include a sequence-preserving control.} When comparing a natural/entity partition against a synthetic non-IID partition, add a \emph{run-level} version of the synthetic partition (same skew, intact sequences). If the gap vanishes (as here), the effect was sequence corruption, not heterogeneity.
  \item \textbf{Separate the axes.} Vary heterogeneity and entity-coherence independently of sequence integrity (e.g., \lstinline|run_random| and \lstinline|run_dirichlet|) before attributing an effect to ``client heterogeneity.''
\end{enumerate}

Our \lstinline|run_random| / \lstinline|run_dirichlet| partition modes (released with the benchmark) implement items~3--4. Applying this checklist to our own data turns a striking ``inverted heterogeneity'' into a corrected, mundane result --- FL on O-RAN slice-SLA is robust to client heterogeneity once sequences are kept intact --- and we conjecture the same audit would revise heterogeneity effects in other federated time-series benchmarks that partition by row.

\section{Limitations and Threats to Validity}\label{sec:limits}

We enumerate the limitations and threats to the validity of the Section~\ref{sec:intro} contributions. Each item is paired with the contribution(s) it most threatens.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{L1 (threats contributions 1, 4): Single-hardware energy reporting.} All energy numbers are NVML measurements on a single RTX~4080 (sm\_89). Energy on consumer-tier mobile GPUs (RTX 30-series at lower TGP), data-centre accelerators (A100, H100), and sparsity-aware accelerators (Loihi-2) will differ in absolute magnitude and possibly in the architectural ranking. The headline $10\times$ LSTM-vs-Spiking energy ratio is a property of the dense GPU $+$ $T = 5$ spiking-simulation regime; on sparsity-aware hardware the ratio would shrink (prior centralized estimate $\approx 0.49$). A cross-GPU robustness sweep (T4, A100) is open future work.

  \item \textbf{L2 (threats contributions 2, 3): main 5-algorithm baseline excludes 2024--2026 sharpness-aware FL; FedSWA add-on in Section~\ref{sec:why-no-fedswa}; FedSCAM + FedGMT 60-cell extension in Section~\ref{sec:sam-family-empirical}.} FedSWA~\cite{Liu2025_FedSWA} is empirically evaluated in Section~\ref{sec:why-no-fedswa} (5-seed paired bootstrap on LSTM $\times$ natural-by-BS); the result marginally reverses the Section~\ref{sec:related-sharp} directional prediction but FedSWA's gap over FedAvg ($+0.0022$) lands well within the $+0.016$ envelope Section~\ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling} establishes for the preregistered baseline. FedSCAM~\cite{FedSCAM2026} and FedGMT~\cite{FedGMT2025} are empirically evaluated in Section~\ref{sec:sam-family-empirical} (60 cells V100, 5-seed paired bootstrap on LSTM $\times$ 6 partitions); FedSCAM finds a $+0.0092$ AUC niche at $\alphadir = 0.10$ (CI$_{95}$ excludes zero) but ties FedAvg at the other five partitions, while FedGMT is strictly dominated by FedAdam at all six partitions ($\Delta \in [-0.027, -0.005]$ AUC, all CI$_{95}$ exclude zero negatively). The Section~\ref{sec:related-sharp} mechanism prediction survives for FedGMT but is partially refuted for FedSCAM at the moderate-heterogeneity cell --- per-client SAM-radius modulation extracts signal that FedAdam's server-side $(m, v)$ does not capture. FedMoSWA~\cite{FedMoSWA2025} empirical evaluation on Colosseum/ColO-RAN remains deferred to follow-up work; the mechanism-based argument in Sections~\ref{sec:related-sharp} + \ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling} + \ref{sec:why-fedadam-saturates} predicts it would perform within the FedAdam $+0.016$ envelope in our regime. Regarding FedBN~\cite{Li2021_FedBN}, often suggested as the feature-shift FL baseline: our 3 backbones (LSTM, Mamba, Spiking-SSM) intentionally omit BatchNorm. FedBN's defining server-side rule --- skip BatchNorm parameters during aggregation --- therefore reduces bit-exactly to FedAvg's complete aggregation on these architectures (proof in \lstinline|artifacts/audit/fedbn_reduces_to_fedavg.md|). \textbf{We additionally ran a 30-cell empirical confirmation} (3 archs $\times$ FedBN $\times$ natural-by-BS $\times$ 10 seeds, 100 rounds, RTX~4080) and verified the reduction on the trained outputs. Per-architecture paired-bootstrap CI$_{95}$ ($n_{\mathrm{boot}}=10\,000$, matching the Section~\ref{sec:stats} protocol) on the (FedBN $-$ Phase-5-FedAvg) per-seed test-AUC delta: LSTM $\Delta = 0$ $[0, 0]$ (bit-exact 10/10 seeds); Mamba $\Delta = +1.0\mathrm{e}{-}6$ $[-4\mathrm{e}{-}6, +8\mathrm{e}{-}6]$; Spiking $\Delta = +4.9\mathrm{e}{-}4$ $[-4.4\mathrm{e}{-}3, +4.3\mathrm{e}{-}3]$. \textbf{All 3 architectures CI$_{95}$ include zero} --- empirically equivalent to FedAvg. The Spiking CI width ($\sim 9\mathrm{e}{-}3$) is wider than the inter-algorithm gaps reported in Section~\ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling} (FedAdam-vs-FedAvg $\approx 0.006$--$0.016$ AUC), but this BF16-numerical-noise floor affects all algorithm-pair comparisons equally and is captured in Section~\ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling}'s paired-bootstrap CIs by the same mechanism. A 3-cell diagnostic further isolates the Spiking-arch $\sim 3.5\mathrm{e}{-}3$ cross-time delta as environmental drift over the 6 weeks between Phase~5 and the FedBN sweep (likely CUDA driver / PyTorch / matmul-order changes; BF16 numerics are not bit-reproducible across such updates), not algorithmic difference. FedAvg is already in our 5-algorithm baseline. The reported FedBN benefit on cellular feature-skew tasks (arXiv:2508.08479~\cite{arXiv2508_08479} reports $+0.01$--$0.03$ AUC over FedAvg) is BN-specific and structurally not transferable to no-BN backbones. \textbf{Beyond the literal FedBN-reduces-to-FedAvg proof, we additionally tested the FedBN-style personalisation principle directly:} take each Phase 5 LSTM/Mamba/Spiking $\times$ FedAvg $\times$ natural-by-BS checkpoint, per BS fine-tune $K=200$ Adam steps on local train data with the same hyperparameters as Phase 5 client step, then evaluate per-BS personalised vs global AUC on the OOD test split. Across 15 (arch $\times$ seed) cells $\times$ 7 BS each $= 105$ per-BS observations: mean $\Delta_{\mathrm{personalised}-\mathrm{global}} = -0.0025$ AUC. The cross-observation std ($0.0036$) is dominated by the per-arch mean spread (Spiking pulls down the cross-arch mean); the \textbf{within-arch} stds across seeds are LSTM $4\mathrm{e}{-}4$, Mamba $2\mathrm{e}{-}4$, Spiking $3\mathrm{e}{-}3$ --- i.e.\ individual fine-tunes are an order of magnitude tighter than the cross-observation std suggests. Per-arch means: LSTM $-0.0007$, Mamba $-0.0014$, Spiking $-0.0053$. \textbf{0/15 cells show positive personalisation $\Delta$ at the cell level} --- this simple per-BS fine-tuning operationalisation of feature-shift personalisation (a BN-independent reading of the FedBN-spirit) does \emph{not} improve test-AUC on this dataset across the 3 backbones tested; we do not generalise this to all FedBN-style or feature-shift personalisation methods. Source: \lstinline|artifacts/r2_post_hoc_per_bs_finetune/aggregated.json| $+$ \lstinline|experiments/run_r2_post_hoc_per_bs_finetune.py|. This more directly addresses the ``most directly relevant feature-shift FL baseline is missing'' concern and supports the Section~\ref{sec:inverted-alpha} mechanism reading that the global FedAvg model is already optimal for the natural-by-BS partition. MOON~\cite{Li2021_MOON} is structurally deferred (preregistered) because its \lstinline|forecaster_encode_fn| does not generalise cleanly to selective-SSM or spiking-SSM activations.

  \item \textbf{L3 (threats contribution 1): Round count is short by image-classification standards.} We use 100 communication rounds $\times$ 50 max-steps per round $\times$ 5 clients sampled per round $=$ \textbf{25\,000 total gradient-steps per cell} (the figure that matches the audit-corrected centralised budget from our prior centralized work). Per individual client, each client is sampled with probability $5/7$ per round and executes 50 steps when sampled, so the \emph{expected} per-client gradient-step count is $100 \times (5/7) \times 50 \approx 3\,571$ steps per client per cell. Both numbers are short relative to the 1\,000-round runs typical of FedSWA / FedSCAM evaluations. The predicted-flatness argument holds at this round count; whether it relaxes at longer round counts is open. A 1\,000-round LSTM $\times$ FedAvg $\times$ IID extrapolation experiment is cheap ($\sim 3$~hr GPU on RTX~4080) and is a candidate future ablation.

  \item \textbf{L4 (threats contribution 1): 7-client natural-deployment scale.} All experiments use 7 clients --- the natural base-station count of the public ColO-RAN release, not a researcher choice --- at $5/7 = 71\%$ participation per round. Statements about FL behaviour at the cross-device 1\,000-client / $10\%$ participation regime~\cite{Hsu2019_Dirichlet} do not generalise from this study. The Section~\ref{sec:why-fedadam-saturates} argument explicitly acknowledges this: contribution~2's flatness is a property of high-participation FL at small-$N$ (here, the dataset-imposed $N = 7$), not of FL in general. Larger-scale ColO-RAN releases or alternative O-RAN testbeds (e.g., OpenAirInterface 5G, srsRAN) could let future work probe the cross-device limit, subject to non-trivial engineering effort to align telemetry format with our preprocessing pipeline (OAI 5G's logging differs substantially; srsRAN lacks integrated FL training infrastructure).

  \item \textbf{L5 (threats contribution 5): Reproducibility infrastructure not yet released.} The full 4-axis table maps deployment configurations to AUC / F1 / energy across 90 groups; the contribution~5 reproducibility claim depends on the Section~\ref{sec:repro} release of the spec-driven YAML pipeline $+$ paired-bootstrap statistics $+$ Croissant metadata $+$ Zenodo DOI under AGPL-3.0. At submission time the artefact is mature in \lstinline|src/fl_oran/|, \lstinline|experiments/specs/|, and \lstinline|tests/| but the Section~\ref{sec:repro} reproducibility section is staged for the camera-ready release; we will provide a Dockerfile $+$ \lstinline|requirements.lock| $+$ demo notebook in the supplementary archive.

  \item \textbf{L6 (threats contribution 4): Pareto Spiking arm assumes hardware sparsity unavailable on RTX~4080.} The Section~\ref{sec:arch-leverage} architecture-leverage claim treats Spiking as a distinct vertical band at $\sim 41$\,kJ/cell. On the deployed sparsity-aware target the Spiking arm's energy would compress (per Section~\ref{sec:arch-leverage-discussion} and prior centralized analysis), and the Pareto envelope between LSTM and Spiking would tighten substantively. Operators targeting commodity GPU should read the Section~\ref{sec:arch-leverage} ranking as-is; operators targeting Loihi-2 / NorthPole should read the ranking with a $2$--$3\times$ downward correction on Spiking energy.

  \item \textbf{L7 (was ``mechanism open''; now RESOLVED, Section~\ref{sec:run-level}): the inverted-$\alphadir$ is a sequence-integrity artifact.} The \lstinline|random_split| ablation (Section~\ref{sec:ablation}) could not separate bs-coherence from sequence integrity because it breaks both at once. The run-level controls (Section~\ref{sec:run-level}) resolve it: \lstinline|run_random| (intact runs, bs-shuffled) ties natural-by-BS (paired CI$_{95}$ $[-0.0003, +0.0001]$) and run-level Dirichlet holds AUC flat across $\alphadir$, so the inversion is per-client sliding-window corruption introduced by row-level partitioning, not a structural property. Residual scope: the run-level controls are LSTM-primary (Mamba / Spiking-SSM confirmed in Table~\ref{tab:run-vs-row}) on a single dataset; cross-dataset replication is open future work.

  \item \textbf{L17 (scope of the sequence-integrity pitfall): benchmark-construction-specific.} The artifact arises specifically when heterogeneity is \emph{synthesised} by row-level partitioning of a pooled time series followed by per-client windowing (the standard FL-benchmark recipe). Real deployments with naturally-partitioned entity clients (each gNB holding its own intact stream) do not incur it; our recommendation to partition by entity is the deployment-correct choice and trivial in situ. The generality claim is therefore about \emph{benchmarking methodology}; per-paper susceptibility of specific prior benchmarks depends on their exact partition-then-window order, which we have not audited individually.

  \item \textbf{L8 (threats contribution 2): SCAFFOLD interaction not exhaustively swept.} The Section~\ref{sec:mamba-scaffold} catastrophic-interaction finding uses canonical SCAFFOLD hyperparameters. A SCAFFOLD client-LR / control-variate-EMA grid sweep on Mamba $\times$ $\alphadir = 0.10$ would establish whether the failure is recoverable in some hyperparameter region; we report the interaction with canonical hyperparameters to establish the existence of the failure mode rather than its precise envelope.

  \item \textbf{L9 (threats Sections~\ref{sec:dataset}, \ref{sec:results}, \ref{sec:why-heterogeneity-helps}): \lstinline|pos_weight_split=train| is one of three valid choices.} The BCE loss reweighting is globally pooled across all clients (Section~\ref{sec:why-heterogeneity-helps}), so the \emph{direction} of all findings is invariant to this choice; full alternative-computation discussion in App.~C.1.

  \item \textbf{L10 (threats contribution 5 reproducibility): bf16 mixed-precision training versus fp32.} All Phase~5 cells use bf16; we do not report an fp32 verification cell. Full numerical-precision argument in App.~C.2.

  \item \textbf{L11 (threats contribution 1, Section~\ref{sec:why-heterogeneity-helps}): Per-client compute budget split as 100 rounds $\times$ 50 max-steps versus alternative splits.} We treat round count as a fixed reproducibility anchor; whether the Section~\ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling} FedAdam-saturates-headroom finding survives at alternative splits is open. Full discussion in App.~C.3.

  \item \textbf{L12 (threats contribution 1): SLA-threshold sensitivity not exhaustively swept.} The $10\%$ BLER threshold is the canonical ColO-RAN SLA gate~\cite{Polese2022}; a $\{5\%, 15\%, 20\%\}$ sweep is the highest-leverage open ablation after Section~\ref{sec:ablation}. Full discussion in App.~C.4.

  \item \textbf{L13 (threats contribution 5 reproducibility): Bootstrap CI uses percentile interval, not BCa.} At our $n = 10$ paired seeds with approximately symmetric per-seed delta distributions, the BCa bias-correction term is bounded by $O(1/\sqrt{n}) \approx 0.32$ standard errors --- smaller than seed $\sigma$ on every reported finding except Section~\ref{sec:mamba-scaffold}'s Mamba$\times$SCAFFOLD. Full justification in App.~C.5.

  \item \textbf{L14 (threats contribution 2): FedAdam hyperparameter sensitivity untested.} $\beta_1 = 0.9$, $\beta_2 = 0.99$, $\eta_{\mathrm{server}} = 0.01$ follow Reddi et al.~2021~\cite{Reddi2021_FedAdam} preregistered values; $(\beta_1, \beta_2, \eta_{\mathrm{server}})$ sensitivity sweep deferred. Full discussion in App.~C.6.

We ran a leave-one-traffic-config-out (LOTO) cluster bootstrap on the LSTM/Mamba/Spiking $\times$ FedAvg $\times$ natural-by-BS Phase~5 cells (10 seeds $\times$ 3 test \lstinline|tr| configs each, inference-only, \lstinline|experiments/run_p2_loto_cluster_bootstrap.py|): see Table~\ref{tab:loto-variance}.

\textbf{For absolute test-AUC claims} (per-cell numbers in Section~\ref{sec:results} tables), the standard CI$_{95}$ is 4--28$\times$ too tight --- though the qualitative conclusions survive because the inverted-$\alphadir$ gap ($\sim 0.17$ AUC, Section~\ref{sec:inverted-alpha}) and the architecture-band separation ($\sim 0.06$--$0.07$ AUC, Section~\ref{sec:arch-leverage}) dwarf even the cluster-CI$_{95}$ widths ($\leq 0.023$ AUC). \textbf{For paired-delta claims} (Section~\ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling} FedAdam-vs-FedAvg, Section~\ref{sec:mamba-scaffold} Mamba$\times$SCAFFOLD, the 270 paired-bootstrap distributions), the per-test-config noise \emph{partially} cancels per-pair: both algorithms in a contrast are evaluated on the same fixed \lstinline|tr| split, so the additive component of $\sigma_{\mathrm{tr}}$ that is shared between the two algorithms drops out of the paired delta. Differential privacy (per-client DP-SGD), secure aggregation (cryptographic gradient masking), and gradient-leakage hardening are all compatible with the FedAvg/FedAdam pipeline used here but are out of scope for this benchmark --- the paper's contribution is the architecture $\times$ algorithm $\times$ heterogeneity sweep, not a privacy mechanism evaluation. Operators deploying federated SLA prediction should pair the chosen algorithm with a privacy mechanism whose threat-model matches the operator's policy.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented the first comprehensive 4-axis federated-learning benchmark on the publicly-available Colosseum/ColO-RAN testbed for slice-level SLA-violation prediction, sweeping \{LSTM, Mamba, Spiking-SSM\} $\times$ \{FedAvg, FedProx, FedAdam, SCAFFOLD, FedDyn\} $\times$ \{natural-by-BS, Dirichlet $\alphadir \in [0.05, 5.0]$\} $\times$ 10 seeds $= 900$ cells with NVML idle-baseline-subtracted training-energy measurement on commodity-tier RTX~4080, augmented by a 15-cell V100 ablation that tests the leading mechanism hypothesis for the headline inverted-$\alphadir$ finding.

The principal finding is methodological: \textbf{the apparent ``inverted heterogeneity'' --- natural-by-BS seemingly beating every Dirichlet $\alphadir$ across all architectures and algorithms --- is a sequence-integrity artifact of row-level non-IID partitioning, not a structural property of RAN telemetry.} Controlled run-level partitioning experiments (Section~\ref{sec:run-level}) show that breaking bs-coherence while keeping each run's sliding windows intact (\lstinline|run_random|) costs ${\sim}0$ AUC, and a run-level Dirichlet at matched $\alphadir$ holds AUC flat at the natural-by-BS level, while the row-level deficit tracks window-fragmentation severity (the per-client contiguous-window fraction rank-correlates perfectly with AUC). Because row-level Dirichlet partitioning is the de-facto FL-benchmark default and is routinely transplanted to time-series tasks, this pitfall plausibly affects other federated time-series benchmarks; Section~\ref{sec:checklist} distils a partitioning checklist. The deployment recommendation --- partition by natural gNB --- is unchanged and trivial (it preserves each cell's time series); what does not survive is the scientific reading that RAN telemetry carries special cell-conditional structure defeating standard FL heterogeneity. The three secondary findings (FedAdam saturating algorithmic headroom; Mamba $\times$ SCAFFOLD catastrophic interaction; architecture leverage dominating algorithm leverage on energy) are detailed in Section~\ref{sec:intro} contributions 2--4 and Sections~\ref{sec:fedadam-ceiling}--\ref{sec:arch-leverage}; we do not restate the numbers here.

The community-level implication is the one operationalisable insight beyond the per-cell numbers: standard FL practice --- \emph{partition clients by inducing artificial uniformity, then deploy sharpness-aware aggregation to suppress the heterogeneity-induced noise} --- is the wrong protocol for O-RAN edge-FL when natural client structure (one gNB, one client) is already heterogeneous in the prediction-relevant axis. The recommendation, in our setup, reverses: \textbf{preserve the natural client partition, and require synthetic re-partitioning to be justified by a specific operational constraint} (e.g., privacy-aware aggregation) rather than adopted as a default benchmarking convention. For O-RAN edge-FL practitioners, the operational recommendation is concrete: deploy on the natural base-station partition rather than re-shuffle clients to enforce IID-like uniformity; select architecture for the energy-AUC trade-off (LSTM at the low-energy frontier on commodity GPU; Spiking-SSM remains a research direction pending sparsity-aware accelerator availability --- its $10\times$ higher energy on dense GPU is hardware-specific, not a fundamental architectural property, and a deployment recommendation must wait until target neuromorphic / sparsity-aware hardware is empirically characterised, see Section~\ref{sec:arch-leverage-discussion} + Section~\ref{sec:limits} L1, L6); choose algorithm based on operational constraints (state size, communication overhead, straggler tolerance) rather than chase $\leq 0.02$ AUC gains, with the explicit caveat that selective-SSM $\times$ control-variate combinations need partition-aware validation before deployment.

Upon paper acceptance, we will release the full benchmark artefact --- spec-driven YAML pipeline, pre-flight algorithm validation, paired-bootstrap statistical inference, NVML training-energy instrumentation, Croissant dataset metadata, and a Zenodo-archived release tag --- as a reference baseline for further FL-on-O-RAN research.

Five open directions, each detailed in App.~D:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Mechanism isolation re-run} --- \lstinline|sub_per_bs=2| with \lstinline|clients-per-round=10| to disentangle slice-mixing from per-round participation in the Section~\ref{sec:per-bs-ablation} residual gap, in line with the broader non-IID-impact methodology of Jimenez-Gutierrez et al.~\cite{JimenezGutierrez2025_NonIID} and the proximity-partition framing of ProFed~\cite{ProFed2025}.

  \item \textbf{Privacy + neuromorphic} --- over-the-air DP~\cite{arXiv2510_23463}, sparse-RNN edge acceleration~\cite{arXiv2502_01330}, and spiking-NN low-power edge sensing~\cite{arXiv2604_27004}.
